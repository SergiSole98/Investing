## Smart Money: Gold (XAU)
Scope: major institutional actors with structural price influence; public evidence only

1. People's Bank of China (PBoC) — Category: central bank | Influence: high
   Evidence: PBoC monthly reserve disclosures via SAFE, ongoing through Q1 2026
   Notes: Largest reported central bank gold buyer since 2022; consecutive monthly additions to reserves totaling hundreds of tonnes reshape physical demand structure.

2. Reserve Bank of India (RBI) — Category: central bank | Influence: high
   Evidence: RBI monthly bulletin and IMF IFS data, ongoing through Q1 2026
   Notes: Sustained multi-year accumulation programme making India one of the top central bank buyers by volume.

3. Central Bank of Turkey (CBRT) — Category: central bank | Influence: high
   Evidence: CBRT weekly balance sheet disclosures, ongoing through 2025-2026
   Notes: Aggressive gold accumulation as part of reserve diversification away from USD; among the top three central bank buyers globally.

4. National Bank of Poland (NBP) — Category: central bank | Influence: high
   Evidence: NBP official reserve reports, 2023-2025
   Notes: Publicly stated target to raise gold to 20% of reserves; large and consistent purchases over multiple quarters.

5. SPDR Gold Shares (GLD) — Category: ETF / asset manager proxy | Influence: high
   Evidence: World Gold Council ETF holdings tracker; daily AUM and tonnage disclosures
   Notes: Largest gold ETF globally; inflows and outflows directly affect physical market via authorized participant creation/redemption.

6. iShares Gold Trust (IAU) — Category: ETF / asset manager proxy | Influence: medium
   Evidence: BlackRock fund disclosures; World Gold Council ETF holdings data
   Notes: Second-largest gold ETF; institutional allocation shifts into or out of IAU move meaningful tonnage.

7. Bridgewater Associates — Category: hedge fund / macro | Influence: medium
   Evidence: SEC 13F filings (quarterly), most recent Q4 2025
   Notes: Largest macro hedge fund with a publicly documented all-weather allocation to gold and gold-linked instruments.

8. Ray Dalio / Dalio Family Office — Category: family office / macro | Influence: medium
   Evidence: Public commentary and 13F filings showing GLD positions, 2024-2025
   Notes: High-profile gold advocate whose public stance and large positions influence institutional sentiment.

9. Shanghai Gold Exchange (SGE) institutional flows — Category: exchange / market structure | Influence: high
   Evidence: SGE weekly withdrawal and delivery data, ongoing through 2026
   Notes: SGE physical withdrawals proxy Chinese wholesale demand; persistent premium over London price signals structural Eastern demand.

10. Commodity Trading Advisors (CTA aggregate) — Category: CTA / managed futures | Influence: medium
    Evidence: CFTC COT disaggregated report, managed money category, weekly
    Notes: CTA net long positioning in COMEX gold futures drives short-term momentum; extreme positioning flags potential reversals.
