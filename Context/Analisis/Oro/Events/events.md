- [2026-04-10 08:30 ET] US CPI (Consumer Price Index) for March 2026 — Actor: Bureau of Labor Statistics (BLS)
  March 2026 CPI is scheduled for 8:30 a.m. ET on April 10; CPI nowcasts as of early April sit at 3.71% YoY (up from March's 3.25%), driven by Iran-war energy price shocks. Consensus is for a material uptick in headline CPI due to energy pass-through; core CPI trajectory is the key variable. A hot print (>3.8% YoY headline or >3.5% core) would push real rates higher, strengthen the dollar, and pressure gold toward $4,500 support; a cool print (<3.5% headline) would dramatically revive rate-cut expectations and catalyze a gold re-rating toward $5,000, consistent with where Goldman Sachs and UBS targets sit. This is the single highest-impact scheduled event for gold in the April 8–15 window. CTA managed money (identified in smart_money.md) is positioned to rapidly re-enter gold longs on a dovish CPI surprise.

  PPI for March 2026 is scheduled for 8:30 a.m. ET on April 14; PPI leads CPI by 2–4 weeks and is particularly sensitive to energy cost pass-through, making the Iran-conflict energy shock directly visible in this release. A hot PPI print would reinforce the "stagflationary" narrative embedded in the FOMC minutes and build the case against rate cuts, further headwind for gold; a cool PPI would signal energy cost absorption and boost rate-cut expectations. PPI's gold impact is typically secondary to CPI but can materially shift rate expectations especially in an energy-shock context.

Each Trump statement, Israeli military action, and Hormuz shipping report over the next 7 days functions as a micro-catalyst within this overarching event framework.
