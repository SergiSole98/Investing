## Events: Gold (XAU/USD)
Scope: scheduled catalysts, horizon 0-7d; events with confirmed date this calendar week

- [2026-03-31 10:00 ET] JOLTS Job Openings (February 2026 data) — Actor: US Bureau of Labor Statistics
  January JOLTS showed the labor market still resilient. A higher-than-expected reading would reinforce the "no rate cuts" narrative, strengthening the dollar and raising real yields, which is negative for gold. A significant miss would revive easing expectations and support XAU. Prior: January 2026 data released February 5. Direct channel: labor market tightness feeds into Fed rate expectations and real yield trajectory.

- [2026-04-01 10:00 ET] ISM Manufacturing PMI (March 2026 data) — Actor: Institute for Supply Management
  February ISM Manufacturing PMI printed at 52.4, indicating expansion. A reading above consensus would reinforce stagflation fears (strong activity + persistent inflation from oil shock), which is ambiguous for gold: stagflation historically supports gold but current high real yields offset. A contractionary surprise below 50 would signal recession risk, potentially triggering safe-haven gold demand. Direct channel: manufacturing activity level shapes Fed policy expectations and risk sentiment.

- [2026-04-03 08:30 ET] Nonfarm Payrolls (March 2026 data) — Actor: US Bureau of Labor Statistics
  The March employment report is the most impactful scheduled release this week for gold. A strong payrolls number would cement expectations that the Fed keeps rates at current levels through mid-2026, pushing the dollar higher and gold lower. A weak print would open the door to rate cut speculation, supporting gold. The Iran war's impact on energy-sector employment and broader hiring decisions adds uncertainty to this release. Prior: February NFP released March 6.

- [2026-04-01 TBD] OPEC+ Joint Ministerial Monitoring Committee (JMMC) meeting — Actor: OPEC+
  The JMMC reviews compliance and production targets. With Brent above $100 due to the Iran war disrupting supply, any decision to increase production quotas would push oil lower, easing inflation expectations and supporting gold. A hold or further cut signals would keep oil elevated, sustaining the higher-for-longer rate narrative that pressures gold. Direct channel: oil price affects inflation expectations, which feed into Fed rate path and real yields.

- [2026-03-28 15:30 ET] CFTC Commitments of Traders report (week ending March 24) — Actor: CFTC
  The weekly COT report will show updated managed money positioning in COMEX gold futures. The prior report showed 102,236 net long contracts. A further reduction in net longs would signal continued CTA deleveraging and potential for additional mechanical selling if key technical levels break. An increase would suggest speculative demand is stabilizing. Direct channel: CTA positioning drives short-term momentum and can trigger cascading liquidation at support levels.

- [2026-03-27 to 2026-04-03 ongoing] US-Iran ceasefire negotiations — Actor: US White House / Iran
  Not a single-date event but an active negotiation with potential for headline-driven moves at any moment. Iran rejected the 15-point US peace proposal on March 26. Any credible ceasefire agreement would collapse oil prices rapidly (potentially $15-20/bbl), ease inflation expectations, revive rate-cut hopes, and remove the war premium from markets. This would be strongly bullish for gold via the real-yield channel. Continued escalation keeps oil elevated and gold under pressure from the rate channel despite safe-haven flows.
