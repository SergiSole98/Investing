## News: Gold (XAU/GCM6)
Scope: impact-driven items (operational, regulatory, strategic, commodity/macro), recognized sources only, horizon 4h–7d; sources within last 7 days

- [2026-04-07] US and Iran agree to two-week ceasefire; Strait of Hormuz to reopen — Source: CNBC / NPR / Reuters
  Trump agreed to suspend planned attacks on Iranian infrastructure for two weeks, contingent on Iran's "COMPLETE, IMMEDIATE, and SAFE OPENING" of the Strait of Hormuz; Pakistan mediated the deal announced April 7 evening ET. Gold initially surged 3.1% to a three-week high near $4,850–4,857 on the announcement before retreating to ~$4,705–$4,800 as investors parsed the fragility of the truce. The ceasefire reduces the acute geopolitical "war premium" baked into gold, but the deal shows cracks already (Israel continued strikes in Lebanon; Iran and Gulf nations reported attacks), preserving residual safe-haven support. The ceasefire expires in approximately two weeks, creating a hard deadline that functions as a binary catalyst for gold going into April 21–22.

- [2026-04-08] FOMC Minutes (March 17–18 meeting) released — risks now "two-sided"; rate hike as likely as cut — Source: Kitco News / Federal Reserve
  The Fed released minutes from its March 2026 meeting on April 8, showing officials deeply divided: some favor cuts if inflation eases, others argue for holding rates steady, and a minority flagged that rate hikes could become necessary if Iran-driven energy inflation proves persistent. The minutes explicitly cited Iran conflict impacts and geopolitical uncertainty as major risks to both inflation (upside) and economic growth (downside). For gold, this hawkish tilt is a near-term headwind — CME FedWatch shows 0% probability of a cut at the April meeting — but any subsequent CPI print that surprises to the downside could rapidly re-price rate-cut expectations and boost gold. The "two-sided" framing removed what had been a structural dovish tailwind that supported gold prices in Q1 2026.

- [2026-04-08] Ceasefire shows early cracks: Israel strikes Beirut, Gulf nations report attacks — Source: NPR / Washington Post
  Despite the US-Iran ceasefire announced April 7, Israel launched its largest attack on Lebanon since the war began, striking multiple neighborhoods in central Beirut during morning rush hour on April 8; Netanyahu's office stated the ceasefire does not cover Lebanon. Iran and several Gulf Arab countries also reported attacks, signaling the truce is non-universal and inherently fragile. Gold's retreat from $4,857 to ~$4,705–$4,800 reflects partial but not full reduction of war premium; the persistence of regional violence provides a safety floor preventing a complete collapse of geopolitical safe-haven demand. Traders must price a dual scenario: ceasefire holding (gold declines further toward $4,500–4,600) vs. ceasefire breakdown (gold surges back toward $5,000+).

- [2026-04-05] Gold declines 0.5% as Trump threatens Iran escalation ahead of Hormuz deadline — Source: Bloomberg
  Gold fell 0.5% on April 5 as Trump threatened further attacks on Iran's civilian infrastructure and suggested Iran could be "taken out" before a deadline he imposed for the Strait of Hormuz to reopen. Dollar strengthened on the hawkish speech, pressuring non-yielding bullion; safe-haven buying from Asian investors partially offset the decline. The episode illustrated gold's volatile sensitivity to Trump's Iran rhetoric — acting as both a geopolitical safe haven and a USD-denominated commodity subject to dollar strength from conflict escalation narratives.

- [2026-04-02] Gold spot plunges 2.7%–3.6% to $4,587–$4,622 after Trump's Iran speech — Source: CNBC / FXStreet
  On April 2, gold reversed a four-session rally, dropping as much as 3.6% after Trump's nationally televised speech declared US military goals "nearing completion" yet pledged continued escalation, creating a paradox that strengthened the dollar (DXY surged) while eliminating the imminent "resolution premium." CPI nowcasts jumped to 3.71% (from March's 3.25%) driven by energy shocks, fueling Fed hawks and further suppressing gold via the real-rates channel. A "Warsh Shock" — hawkish signals from former Fed Governor Kevin Warsh, widely seen as the next Fed Chair candidate — compounded the sell-off; gold had been near $4,750+ before the session. This move established the ~$4,580–$4,622 range as a key support zone to watch if geopolitical tailwinds unwind further.

- [2026-04-03] Turkey dumps ~120 tonnes of gold reserves; GLD and IAU record combined $5.4bn ETF outflows — Source: Invezz / National Today / SSGA Monthly Gold Monitor
  The Central Bank of Turkey sold approximately 120 tonnes of gold over two weeks to address an energy crisis and currency (lira) pressure driven by the Iran war's energy shock; over half was executed via gold swap transactions rather than outright spot sales. Simultaneously, SPDR GLD shed over $2.8 billion and iShares IAU shed over $2.6 billion in 2026 YTD outflows, with Western institutional investors reducing allocations. Despite this dual selling pressure, gold held above $4,650 as PBoC, emerging market central banks, and Asian retail demand absorbed supply, confirming the structural buyer base. The Turkish selling is classified as a temporary balance-of-payments necessity by analysts, not a structural reversal, but the volume (~120t) is significant relative to normal monthly flows.
