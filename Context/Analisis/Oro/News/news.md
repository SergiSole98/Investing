## News: Gold (XAU/USD)
Scope: price-relevant items, horizon 4h-7d (project); sources within last 7 days

- [2026-03-26] Iran rejects US 15-point ceasefire proposal, oil surges toward $107/bbl — Source: Bloomberg / CNBC
  Tehran formally rejected the US peace framework on March 26, sending Brent crude back above $107 and WTI toward $94. The oil spike reignites inflation expectations, raising the probability the Fed holds rates higher for longer. Higher real yields and a stronger dollar increase the opportunity cost of holding gold, pressuring XAU/USD which dropped sharply to near $4,400 on the session.

- [2026-03-26] Turkey sells and swaps $8 billion in gold since Iran war began — Source: Bloomberg
  The Central Bank of Turkey (CBRT) drew down approximately 60 tonnes of gold in two weeks: 6 tonnes in the week of March 13 and 52.4 tonnes in the week of March 20. Part was sold outright; the majority was used in swap agreements to secure foreign exchange for Lira defense. This is a significant shift from CBRT's multi-year accumulation programme and adds physical supply pressure to the gold market at a time of already elevated selling.

- [2026-03-25] Gold jumps 2% as oil slump eases inflation fears amid Trump-Iran talks — Source: CNBC
  Spot gold rallied nearly 2% to $4,558.81 on March 25 after reports that Washington was working on a proposal to end the Middle East conflict. The decline in oil prices (Brent dropped ~5% to $99.13) tempered inflation fears, briefly reviving expectations for eventual Fed rate cuts. The move was reversed the following day when Iran rejected the proposal.

- [2026-03-24] Gold erases 2026 gains as central bank selling hits headlines — Source: BullionVault
  Russia's central bank continued selling gold in January and February 2026, taking bullion reserves to a 4-year low by weight and raising approximately $2.4 billion. Combined with Turkey's drawdowns and Gulf state reserve movements, the narrative shifted from "central banks are structural buyers" to concern about institutional supply hitting the market. This undermines a key pillar of the gold bull thesis.

- [2026-03-24] Gold ETF outflows accelerate: GLD sheds $1.5 billion in one week — Source: BanklessTimes / Invezz
  The SPDR Gold Shares (GLD) ETF recorded outflows in three consecutive weeks, with the latest week showing over $1.5 billion in redemptions. Year-to-date GLD outflows now exceed $2 billion. Institutional allocators are choosing T-bills yielding 4.9% over zero-yield gold. IAU also experienced outflows. This contrasts with strong global gold ETF inflows earlier in March ($8.6 billion / 92 tonnes globally per WGC data), suggesting the selling is concentrated in US-listed vehicles.

- [2026-03-21] Gold drops nearly 10% in worst weekly rout since 2011 — Source: CNBC
  XAU/USD fell approximately 10% in the week ending March 21, the worst weekly decline since September 2011. The selloff was driven by a hawkish shift in Fed rate-cut projections following hot core PCE data (3.1% YoY in January, highest in nearly two years), rising real yields, dollar strength, and the unwinding of geopolitical safe-haven positioning as ceasefire rumors emerged. Gold hit a 4-month low of $4,099 on March 23.

- [2026-03-20] CFTC COT: managed money net long positioning in gold at 102,236 contracts — Source: CFTC
  The CFTC disaggregated COT report for the week of March 17 showed managed money net long positions at 102,236 contracts, with total open interest at 411,388 contracts. CTA positioning remains net long but has decreased from prior extremes, suggesting some systematic deleveraging has occurred but the position is not yet washed out. Further liquidation risk remains if price breaks below $4,100 support.

- [2026-03-13] Core PCE inflation at 3.1% YoY in January, highest in nearly two years — Source: BEA / Reuters
  The Bureau of Economic Analysis reported core PCE rose 0.4% MoM and 3.1% YoY in January 2026, up from 3.0% in December. Headline PCE moderated to 2.8% from 2.9%. The hot core reading reinforced expectations that the Fed will delay rate cuts, strengthening the dollar and raising real yields. This is a direct negative for gold's near-term price as it reduces the probability of the monetary easing cycle that underpinned the 2024-2025 gold rally.

- [2026-03-07] PBoC extends gold buying, adds 30,000 troy ounces in February — Source: Bloomberg
  The People's Bank of China increased gold reserves by 30,000 troy ounces to 74.22 million fine troy ounces, extending the accumulation round that began in November 2024. While the monthly increment is modest compared to 2023-2024 pace, the continuation signals that China's structural demand remains in place despite record-high prices. This partially offsets the bearish narrative from Turkey and Russia selling.
