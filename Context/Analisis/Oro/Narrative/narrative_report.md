## Key Tension
The central contradiction is between Narrative 1 (structural EM/CB demand provides an inviolable floor, implying limited downside) and Narrative 3 (persistent Iran-driven inflation has triggered genuine two-sided Fed rate risk, removing the easing tailwind that justified the price level well above historical norms). These narratives are not mutually exclusive in isolation, but they create opposing implications for the magnitude and sustainability of any correction: the floor narrative argues dips are shallow and brief; the stagflation narrative argues the current price level (~$4,700) is poorly supported without the rate-cut pillar, meaning a sustained hot CPI + hawkish Fed sequence could overwhelm the EM bid and force a longer correction toward $4,300–$4,450. Resolution is binary and catalyzed by the April 10 CPI print: a cool reading would validate the floor narrative and trigger CTA re-entry (Narrative 4 activation), collapsing the tension bullishly; a hot reading above 3.8% YoY would validate the stagflation narrative and test whether the $4,580 support zone identified in Rank 4 can hold against simultaneous ETF outflow pressure and a hawkish Fed repricing — the outcome of that test would determine whether gold enters a multi-week consolidation or an accelerated correction.
