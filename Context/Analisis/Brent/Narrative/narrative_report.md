## Key Tension
The dominant tension is between Narrative 1/2 (an active physical supply disruption anchored by a hard binary escalation catalyst on April 6) and Narrative 3 (building demand destruction and substantial emergency supply injections implying the current price level is unsustainable once geopolitical risk fades). The resolution mechanism is the April 6 Trump deadline: if the Strait of Hormuz remains closed and the deadline produces no deal, the physical scarcity narrative accelerates and inventory builds become secondary to the supply shortfall; if a deal or extension materializes over the Good Friday weekend, the demand-destruction and structural-surplus evidence reasserts rapidly with no market open available to absorb the move until Monday April 7, creating maximum gap-risk potential. The OPEC+ meeting on April 5 — itself carrying high intentionality risk — introduces a further variable the day before the deadline: a suspension of the production increase would amplify bullish momentum into the diplomatic binary, while any accelerated-unwind signal would compound downside pressure in a deal scenario, potentially pushing price toward the $66–$85 range Goldman Sachs identifies as the post-conflict normalized level.
