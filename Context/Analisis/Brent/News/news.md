## News: Brent Crude Oil
Scope: price-relevant items, horizon 4h–7d; sources within last 7 days (2026-03-19 to 2026-03-26)

- [2026-03-20] Iraq declares force majeure on all foreign-operated oilfields; Kuwait refineries attacked — Source: Reuters / CNBC / Al Arabiya
  Iraq's oil ministry declared force majeure on all foreign-operated fields on March 20 because it cannot export crude through the Strait of Hormuz; production at Basra Oil Company was cut from 3.3 million bpd to ~900,000 bpd. Simultaneously, drone strikes hit Kuwait's Mina Al-Ahmadi and Mina Abdullah refineries (combined ~730,000 bpd of refining capacity), forcing precautionary shutdowns. Brent surged 3.26% to close at $112.19/bbl. The unresolved status of Iraqi and Kuwaiti export capacity is a persistent near-term upside risk for prompt Brent.

- [2026-03-20] US Treasury issues 30-day sanctions waiver for ~140 million barrels of stranded Iranian crude — Source: CNBC / Washington Post / CNN Politics
  OFAC issued a 30-day general license on March 20 authorizing the sale of Iranian-origin crude already on vessels at sea, valid through April 19, 2026. The waiver is the third such temporary relief in three weeks and is designed to inject ~140 million barrels into global markets to suppress prices. The April 19 expiry is a hard near-term deadline; non-renewal would abruptly re-tighten supply and act as a bullish catalyst, while renewal would extend bearish supply relief.

- [2026-03-20] Goldman Sachs raises Brent forecast; cites largest-ever supply shock — Source: Bloomberg / CNN / Investing.com
  Goldman Sachs raised its 2026 average Brent price forecast to $85/bbl (from $77) and set near-term base-case targets of $105/bbl for March and $115/bbl for April. A six-month Hormuz disruption risk scenario points to $135/bbl. JPMorgan separately projects Brent to average $100/bbl in Q2 2026 before declining to $80 by year-end. These high-profile revisions by major banks reinforce speculative long positioning and can amplify upward moves on bullish headlines.

- [2026-03-23] Trump postpones strikes on Iranian power plants; cites "productive" talks — Source: CNBC / NBC News
  President Trump announced on March 23 that planned US strikes on Iranian energy infrastructure were postponed and described talks as "productive." Brent fell ~11% from ~$112 to ~$99.94/bbl intraday as markets priced in de-escalation. However, Iran denied any direct negotiations were taking place, creating a gap between US and Iranian narratives that keeps the situation volatile and subject to reversal.

- [2026-03-24] Iran rejects US 15-point peace proposal; Brent rebounds sharply to ~$108 — Source: CNBC / Al Jazeera
  Iranian Foreign Minister Abbas Araghchi confirmed on March 24 that Iran rejected the US 15-point peace plan delivered via Pakistani intermediaries. Iran presented five counter-conditions, including cessation of US/Israeli attacks and international recognition of Iranian sovereignty over the Strait of Hormuz. Brent surged 5.66% to $108.01/bbl intraday. The repeated cycle of ceasefire optimism followed by rejection is generating intraday swings of 3–6% and remains the dominant price driver.

- [2026-03-25] EIA weekly inventory report shows large crude build of +6.9 million barrels — Source: US EIA
  The EIA report released March 25 (week ending March 20) showed US crude inventories rose 6.926 million barrels to 456.2 million barrels, versus a consensus estimate of +0.5 million barrels — the fifth consecutive weekly build. Cushing hub stocks rose 3.421 million barrels (largest since January 2023); distillates also built +3.032 million barrels. Consecutive large builds indicate demand destruction from high prices and/or alternative supply routes are showing up in US data, providing a near-term bearish offset to geopolitical supply premiums.

- [2026-03-25] API report: crude build of +2.3 million barrels vs. expected draw of -1.3 million barrels — Source: VT Markets / API
  The API reported a surprise crude inventory build of 2.3 million barrels for the week ended March 20, 3.6 million barrels above consensus, following the prior week's 6.6 million-barrel build. Back-to-back large inventory builds in both API and EIA data suggest the US supply bridge (IEA release + Iranian waiver cargoes) is beginning to offset the Hormuz shortage at the margin, capping upside on any near-term diplomatic relief rally.

- [2026-03-25] Goldman raises recession probability to 30%; Brent oil market in backwardation — Source: Fortune / CNBC
  Goldman Sachs raised its US recession probability to 30% on March 25, citing oil-driven inflation and slower GDP growth. Separately, Brent futures remain in backwardation (prompt premium over deferred), with the front-month trading at ~$105.85–$106.12/bbl on March 26 morning, up $32/bbl year-on-year. The backwardation structure signals the market views the disruption as transitory; a ceasefire breakthrough or second IEA release could rapidly flatten or invert the curve.

- [2026-03-26] Iran-US talks at impasse; Brent stabilizes near $106 — Source: CNBC / NPR / Bloomberg
  As of March 26 morning, Iran continues to deny direct talks with the US while exchanges occur through Pakistani intermediaries. Trump issued a fresh warning to Iran over peace talks. Brent was trading at $105.85–$106.12/bbl, up ~$6.10 from the prior day's close. Any fresh breakthrough or breakdown — including new strikes on Gulf energy infrastructure or a formal Strait reopening agreement — retains the potential to move Brent by $5–10/bbl within hours given thin liquidity and elevated risk premium.
