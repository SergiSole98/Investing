## News: Brent Crude Oil
Scope: price-relevant items, horizon 4h-7d; sources within last 7 days (2026-03-20 to 2026-03-27)

- [2026-03-27] U.S. crude oil hits $100 again after Chinese ships turned away from Strait of Hormuz — Source: CNBC
  Brent crude futures surged to $112.38/bbl on March 27 after Chinese-flagged vessels were denied transit through the Strait of Hormuz by Iranian naval forces, breaking the brief diplomatic calm following Iran's March 26 announcement that ships from five nations (China, Russia, India, Iraq, Pakistan) would be permitted passage. The reversal signals Iran's "toll booth" system remains unpredictable, directly threatening the 17.8 million bpd of oil flows that normally transit the strait. WTI touched $100 intraday.

- [2026-03-26] Trump extends pause on striking Iranian energy plants to April 6 at 8 P.M. ET — Source: Bloomberg / CNBC / NPR
  President Trump extended the deadline for U.S. strikes on Iranian power grid and energy infrastructure to Monday, April 6, 2026 at 8 P.M. Eastern Time, the second extension since the initial 48-hour ultimatum. Iran allowed several oil tankers through the Strait as a goodwill gesture. The April 6 deadline is the single most important binary catalyst for Brent: failure triggers direct strikes on Iranian energy infrastructure; a deal could reopen Hormuz and collapse the $14-18/bbl risk premium estimated by Goldman Sachs.

- [2026-03-26] Israeli military kills IRGC Navy Commander Tangsiri in airstrike — Source: CNBC / Reuters
  Israeli Defense Minister Israel Katz announced that IRGC Navy Commander Alireza Tangsiri, described as directly responsible for the Strait of Hormuz closure, was killed in an airstrike on March 26. Tangsiri commanded the forces enforcing the maritime blockade. His elimination could disrupt the IRGC Navy's command structure but may also provoke retaliatory escalation, adding near-term volatility to Brent.

- [2026-03-26] Iran announces selective transit for ships from five nations; rejects direct U.S. talks — Source: Al Jazeera / CNBC
  Iranian Foreign Minister Abbas Araghchi announced that ships owned by China, Russia, India, Iraq, and Pakistan would be allowed to transit the Strait of Hormuz under an IRGC-controlled vetting system. Simultaneously, Iran's foreign ministry rejected direct bilateral negotiations with the United States, insisting any talks must proceed through intermediaries. Brent dropped from $108 to $105.85 intraday before rebounding. The rejection narrows the diplomatic pathway before the April 6 deadline.

- [2026-03-25] EIA weekly report: U.S. crude inventories rise 6.9 million barrels; fifth consecutive build — Source: EIA
  The EIA report released March 25 (week ending March 20) showed U.S. crude inventories rose 6.926 million barrels to 456.2 million barrels, far exceeding consensus of +0.5 million barrels. Cushing hub stocks rose 3.421 million barrels, the largest weekly increase since January 2023. Distillates also built +3.032 million barrels. Consecutive large builds indicate demand destruction from high prices and alternative supply routes are showing up in U.S. data, providing a bearish offset to geopolitical premiums.

- [2026-03-25] Goldman Sachs raises recession probability to 30%; oil-driven inflation cited — Source: Fortune / Goldman Sachs
  Goldman Sachs raised its U.S. recession probability to 30% on March 25, citing oil-driven inflation and slower GDP growth. Brent futures remain in steep backwardation with prompt-month at ~$106/bbl, up $32/bbl year-on-year. The recession call introduces a demand-destruction narrative that could cap Brent upside if economic deterioration accelerates, even as supply disruptions persist.

- [2026-03-23] Goldman Sachs raises 2026 Brent average forecast to $85/bbl; near-term $115/bbl for April — Source: Bloomberg / Goldman Sachs
  Goldman Sachs raised its 2026 full-year average Brent forecast by $8 to $85/bbl and now expects Brent to average $115/bbl in April, up from $98. A six-month Hormuz disruption risk scenario points to $135/bbl. JPMorgan separately projects Brent at $100/bbl in Q2 before declining to $80 by year-end assuming limited disruption. These high-profile bank revisions reinforce speculative long positioning.

- [2026-03-23] Trump postpones strikes on Iranian power plants; Brent drops ~11% — Source: CNBC / NBC News
  President Trump announced on March 23 that planned U.S. strikes on Iranian energy infrastructure were postponed, describing talks as "productive." Brent fell ~11% from ~$112 to ~$99.94/bbl intraday as markets priced in de-escalation. However, Iran denied any direct negotiations were taking place, creating conflicting narratives that keep the situation volatile.

- [2026-03-20] Iraq declares force majeure; drone attacks hit Kuwaiti refineries — Source: Reuters / CNBC
  Iraq's oil ministry declared force majeure on all foreign-operated fields on March 20 because it cannot export through the Strait of Hormuz; Basra Oil Company production was cut from 3.3 million bpd to ~900,000 bpd. Drone strikes hit Kuwait's Mina Al-Ahmadi and Mina Abdullah refineries (combined ~730,000 bpd capacity), and Saudi Arabia shut Ras Tanura refinery (550,000 bpd) precautionarily. Brent surged 3.26% to close at $112.19/bbl.

- [2026-03-20] U.S. Treasury issues 30-day sanctions waiver for ~140 million barrels of stranded Iranian crude — Source: CNBC / Washington Post
  OFAC issued a 30-day general license authorizing the sale of Iranian-origin crude on vessels at sea, valid through April 19, 2026. The waiver is the third such temporary relief in three weeks and injects ~140 million barrels into global markets to suppress prices. The April 19 expiry is a hard deadline: non-renewal would re-tighten supply (bullish); renewal would extend bearish relief.

- [2026-03-11] IEA coordinated release of 400 million barrels; U.S. SPR contributing 172 million barrels — Source: Bloomberg / DOE / NPR
  The IEA's 32 member nations unanimously agreed to the largest coordinated strategic reserve release in history: 400 million barrels total, with the U.S. DOE contributing 172 million barrels from the SPR. First deliveries began within 9 days. The U.S. SPR holds approximately 415 million barrels; delivery will take approximately 120 days. This is designed to cap price spikes during the Hormuz closure.
