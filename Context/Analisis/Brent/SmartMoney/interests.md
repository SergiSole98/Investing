## Interests Map: Brent Crude Oil
Scope: structural incentives of identified smart money actors

### Andurand Capital Management
Bias: bullish | Confidence: high
Reason: The Andurand Commodities Discretionary Enhanced fund holds an explicit mandate for unlimited directional risk in energy, and public reporting confirms active long oil positioning that generated approximately 20% returns through mid-March 2026 on Brent upside driven by the Strait of Hormuz disruption; this performance creates structural incentive to maintain and defend long exposure.
Near-term deadline: none identified

### Managed Money / Speculative Longs (Aggregate — ICE Brent)
Bias: bullish | Confidence: high
Reason: Aggregated COT data through the week ending March 17 2026 shows net managed-money long positions at a six-year high of 428,000+ lots, with the largest single-week increase since at least 2020; the scale of accumulated long inventory creates strong momentum inertia and structural pressure to sustain upside price direction.
Near-term deadline: Weekly COT release (each Friday) can trigger rapid repositioning if net-long exposure reverses materially.

### Commodity Trading Advisors (CTAs) / Systematic Trend-Following Funds
Bias: bullish | Confidence: high
Reason: Published reports through mid-March 2026 confirm CTA algorithms are 100% long in both WTI and Brent, with trend-following mandates mechanically locked to the prevailing uptrend; this structural design creates automatic buying pressure as long as price remains above key moving-average thresholds, and automatic reversal only on a confirmed trend break.
Near-term deadline: none identified (signal reversal is price-level-contingent, not calendar-driven)

### Saudi Arabia / Saudi Aramco
Bias: bullish | Confidence: high
Reason: Saudi Aramco raised its Official Selling Price for Arab Light by $2.40 for March deliveries — the largest increase since August 2022 — signaling high confidence in near-term demand strength; Saudi Arabia's position as the largest voluntary cutter within OPEC+ (~45% of voluntary cuts) gives it both the structural incentive and the demonstrated commitment to keep supply constrained and support elevated Brent prices.
Near-term deadline: OPEC+ ministerial meeting, April 5 2026 (next formal decision point on production policy).

### OPEC+ Eight Core Producers (Saudi Arabia, UAE, Iraq, Kuwait, Russia, Kazakhstan, Algeria, Gabon)
Bias: bullish | Confidence: high
Reason: The bloc agreed on March 1 2026 to a gradual and minimal production unwind of only 206,000 b/d starting April 2026, explicitly citing market stability; the staged ramp preserves significant voluntary cuts through at least end-March 2026 and aligns the collective fiscal interest of member sovereigns with elevated Brent prices.
Near-term deadline: OPEC+ ministerial meeting, April 5 2026 (next scheduled decision on the pace of production normalization).

### U.S. Department of Energy (DOE)
Bias: bullish | Confidence: medium
Reason: The Trump administration's publicly stated mandate to refill the Strategic Petroleum Reserve from approximately 400 million barrels to capacity makes the DOE a structural physical buyer of crude, providing incremental demand support; active solicitations and documented swap arrangements into 2026 confirm ongoing procurement activity.
Near-term deadline: Bryan Mound delivery windows (Dec 2025–Jan 2026 contracts already awarded); further solicitation cycles are ongoing but specific future award dates are not publicly disclosed.

### Bridgewater Associates
Bias: bullish | Confidence: medium
Reason: Bridgewater's All Weather strategy carries a structural mandate of approximately 37% commodity allocation (including energy) as an inflation and geopolitical hedge; periods of elevated oil prices and volatility trigger rebalancing-driven buying to maintain target weights, creating a persistent non-discretionary bid for Brent.
Near-term deadline: none identified (rebalancing is ongoing and not tied to a single calendar date)

### Goldman Sachs Global Commodities Research
Bias: bullish | Confidence: medium
Reason: Goldman Sachs revised its Brent average forecast upward multiple times through March 2026, projecting $105/bbl base case and $115/bbl under a Hormuz disruption scenario, and its commodity desk flow positioning structurally aligns with published research; bullish published forecasts drive client-order flows that reinforce the prevailing short-term price direction.
Near-term deadline: none identified (Goldman issues rolling research updates; no fixed next publication date documented)

### J.P. Morgan Global Commodities Strategy
Bias: bearish | Confidence: high
Reason: JPM's head of Global Commodities Strategy (Natasha Kaneva) maintains a $60/bbl Brent base case for 2026, characterizing current elevated prices as a temporary war premium layered over a structural surplus backdrop; published bearish research drives client hedging and short-biased positioning that creates overhead resistance and incremental selling pressure near current price levels.
Near-term deadline: none identified (JPM issues rolling research updates; no fixed next publication date documented)

### International Energy Agency (IEA)
Bias: bearish | Confidence: high
Reason: On March 11 2026 the IEA authorized the release of 400 million barrels from member emergency stockpiles — delivering approximately 1.4 mb/d from the U.S. alone over 120 days — representing the largest emergency stock release in history; this coordinated action is structurally opposed to higher prices and signals institutional willingness among IEA members to cap price spikes through supply-side intervention.
Near-term deadline: The 120-day release window runs from approximately mid-March through mid-July 2026; the next IEA Oil Market Report is expected in mid-April 2026.

### Norges Bank Investment Management (NBIM) / Norway Government Pension Fund Global
Bias: neutral | Confidence: high
Reason: NBIM manages a $1.9T diversified multi-asset portfolio and does not take direct commodity futures positions; while Norway's sovereign fiscal revenues benefit from elevated oil prices (the fund's ultimate funding source), NBIM's mandate does not translate into directional Brent exposure, making it a significant barometer of institutional risk sentiment rather than a directional actor.
Near-term deadline: none identified
