## Smart Money: Brent Crude Oil
Scope: major institutional actors with structural price influence; public evidence only

1. OPEC+ (Saudi Arabia / Russia core) — Category: Sovereign Producer Cartel | Influence: high
   Evidence: OPEC+ March 1, 2026 ministerial decision; CNBC, March 1, 2026
   Notes: Controls approximately 40% of global crude supply and sets the physical volume foundation against which Brent is priced; March 2026 output increase of 206,000 b/d directly impacts Brent spot and curve structure.

2. Saudi Aramco / Saudi Ministry of Energy — Category: National Oil Company / Sovereign Producer | Influence: high
   Evidence: Aramco 2025 Annual Results (net income $104.7B); AGBI, March 2026; Saudi Energy Ministry official pricing communiques
   Notes: As the world's largest single crude exporter and the primary setter of official selling prices (OSPs) for Arab Light — a direct Brent differential benchmark — Aramco's pricing decisions transmit immediately into Brent spreads.

3. Goldman Sachs (Commodities Division) — Category: Investment Bank / Commodity Trading Desk | Influence: high
   Evidence: Goldman Sachs Commodities Research note, Brent forecast revised to $85/bbl; Bloomberg, March 23, 2026; InvestingLive, March 22, 2026
   Notes: Goldman's commodities desk (led by Daan Struyven) moves institutional flows through proprietary trading and client facilitation; their March 2026 "largest-ever supply shock" revision was the dominant single catalyst for managed-money re-positioning.

4. Vitol Group — Category: Independent Physical Commodity Trading House | Influence: high
   Evidence: Vitol 2024 Volumes & Review (7.2 mb/d crude and products traded); Vitol.com, 2025
   Notes: The world's largest independent oil trader by volume; Vitol's physical Brent cargo accumulation and disposal directly sets the spot cargo market from which the ICE Brent futures contract settles.

5. Trafigura — Category: Independent Physical Commodity Trading House | Influence: high
   Evidence: Trafigura FY2025 H1 results (~6.8 mb/d traded); FinancialContent / Trafigura press release, November 2025
   Notes: Second-largest physical crude trader globally; Trafigura's swap and futures hedging book in ICE Brent is one of the largest continuous exposures in the swap dealer/other reportables COT categories.

6. Swap Dealers (aggregated, ICE/NYMEX) — Category: Swap Dealer / Bank Commodity Desks | Influence: high
   Evidence: CFTC Disaggregated COT Report, Brent Last Day futures, March 10, 2026: Swap Dealers long 54,594 / short 931 contracts; CFTC.gov
   Notes: Swap dealers collectively hold the largest net long position in reportable categories as of March 10, 2026, reflecting commodity index replication and OTC hedging for producer and consumer clients, giving this cohort outsized influence on roll and term-structure dynamics.

7. Managed Money (aggregated hedge funds / CTAs) — Category: Hedge Fund / CTA Aggregate | Influence: high
   Evidence: CFTC Disaggregated COT Report, Brent Last Day futures, March 10, 2026: Managed Money long 15,344 / short 1,150 / spreading 9,418; CFTC.gov
   Notes: Managed money net positioning is the most-watched sentiment indicator for Brent; abrupt position changes by this category (driven by macro funds and trend-following CTAs) have historically amplified intraday and weekly price moves.

8. Citadel LLC (Commodities Pod) — Category: Multi-Strategy Hedge Fund | Influence: medium
   Evidence: Citadel Commodities business unit disclosure, Citadel.com; Hedgeweek double-digit 2025 return report; commodity expansion noted in sector analysis, 2025
   Notes: Citadel operates dedicated commodities trading pods across energy markets; as one of the largest multi-strategy funds globally ($65B+ AUM), its energy books represent a material fraction of speculative open interest in Brent futures.

9. J.P. Morgan (Commodities Research & Trading) — Category: Investment Bank / Commodity Trading Desk | Influence: medium
   Evidence: J.P. Morgan Oil Price Forecast note (Natasha Kaneva, lead analyst), Brent $58/bbl 2026 base case; JPMorgan.com, published Q1 2026
   Notes: JPMorgan's commodities desk is a primary swap dealer and market-maker in Brent OTC derivatives; the bank's public forecasts shape client positioning mandates and index rebalancing flows across institutional asset managers.

10. Saudi Arabia Public Investment Fund (PIF) — Category: Sovereign Wealth Fund | Influence: medium
    Evidence: PIF 2024 AUM grew 19% to approximately $941B (PIF press release, 2025); PIF holds ~16% stake in Saudi Aramco; Middle East Briefing, 2026
    Notes: PIF's ~16% stake in Aramco ties sovereign wealth flows to Brent revenues; PIF's strategic decisions on Aramco dividend policy and energy sector deployment indirectly anchor medium-term Brent price support expectations among institutional investors.
