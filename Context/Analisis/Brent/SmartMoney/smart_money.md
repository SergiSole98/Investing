## Smart Money: Brent Crude Oil
Scope: major institutional actors with structural price influence; public evidence only

1. Saudi Arabia (via Saudi Aramco / Ministry of Energy) — Category: sovereign producer | Influence: high
   Evidence: OPEC+ ministerial meeting, 1 March 2026; CNBC reporting
   Notes: Swing producer with approximately 2 million bpd spare capacity deployable within 90 days; leads OPEC+ output decisions and sets official selling prices that directly anchor Brent differentials.

2. OPEC+ coalition (Russia, Iraq, UAE, Kuwait, Kazakhstan, Algeria, Oman) — Category: sovereign producer bloc | Influence: high
   Evidence: OPEC communiqué, 4 January 2026; OilPrice.com reporting, March 2026
   Notes: Eight-country group paused production increases through Q1 2026 and approved a 206,000 bpd increase for April 2026, directly managing approximately 40% of global crude supply.

3. U.S. Department of Energy (Strategic Petroleum Reserve) — Category: government / strategic reserve | Influence: high
   Evidence: DOE emergency exchange RFP for 86 million barrels, March 2026; coordinated IEA 400 million barrel release announcement
   Notes: SPR holds approximately 415 million barrels; the 172 million barrel exchange program and coordinated IEA release inject substantial supply during the Strait of Hormuz disruption.

4. China (CNPC / Sinopec / CNOOC — strategic petroleum reserve) — Category: sovereign buyer / strategic reserve | Influence: high
   Evidence: EIA forecast, February 2026; CNBC, 9 March 2026; Columbia CGEP import analysis, 2025
   Notes: Holds an estimated 1.3 billion barrels in strategic and commercial reserves; expected to expand stockpiles by approximately 1 million bpd in 2026, making China the single largest incremental demand source for waterborne crude.

5. Vitol Group — Category: commodity trading house | Influence: high
   Evidence: Vitol 2024 volume disclosure (7.2 million bpd crude and products); Vitol.com, 2025
   Notes: Largest independent oil trader globally; physical Brent cargo accumulation and disposal directly sets the spot cargo market from which the ICE Brent futures contract settles.

6. Trafigura — Category: commodity trading house | Influence: high
   Evidence: Trafigura FY2025 H1 results (approximately 6.8 million bpd traded); secured U.S. licenses for Venezuelan crude, 2026
   Notes: Second-largest physical crude trader; its swap and futures hedging book in ICE Brent is one of the largest continuous exposures in the swap dealer category.

7. Glencore — Category: commodity trading house / producer | Influence: high
   Evidence: Public annual reports; ranked among top three physical commodities traders globally
   Notes: Integrated producer-trader with upstream oil assets and large-scale trading operations that influence physical Brent pricing and arbitrage flows.

8. Managed Money / Hedge Funds (aggregate) — Category: hedge fund / CTA | Influence: high
   Evidence: CFTC COT disaggregated report, 10 March 2026; Energy News Beat, February 2026
   Notes: Net-long Brent positions reached 320,952 lots in late February 2026, the highest in nearly two years; momentum-driven positioning by macro funds and trend-following CTAs amplifies intraday and weekly price moves.

9. Goldman Sachs / J.P. Morgan (Commodities Desks) — Category: investment bank / swap dealer | Influence: medium
   Evidence: Goldman Sachs commodities note revising Brent forecast, March 2026; J.P. Morgan oil price forecast (Brent base case), Q1 2026
   Notes: Primary swap dealers and market-makers in Brent OTC derivatives; their public forecasts and client facilitation flows shape institutional positioning and index rebalancing across asset managers.

10. International Energy Agency member states (coordinated release) — Category: government coalition | Influence: medium
    Evidence: DOE announcement, March 2026; coordinated 400 million barrel release commitment by IEA members
    Notes: Collective strategic reserve releases from member nations represent a joint supply intervention designed to cap price spikes during the Strait of Hormuz closure.
