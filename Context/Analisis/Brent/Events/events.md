## Events: Brent Crude Oil
Scope: scheduled catalysts, horizon 0–7d; events with confirmed date this calendar week

- [2026-03-29, 08:30 ET] U.S. PCE Price Index — Personal Consumption Expenditures, February 2026 — Actor: U.S. Bureau of Economic Analysis (BEA)
  February core PCE (the Fed's preferred inflation gauge) is released today; prior January print was +3.1% YoY, well above the 2.0% target, with consensus expecting core PCE to hold near 3.0–3.1% given energy price pass-through from the Hormuz crisis. A hotter-than-expected print would reinforce higher-for-longer Fed policy, strengthen the USD, and create a demand-side headwind for Brent by raising borrowing costs and compressing growth expectations; a softer-than-expected reading would weaken the dollar and support risk appetite and oil prices into the April 6 deadline.

- [2026-03-31, end of ICE session] ICE Brent Crude May 2026 Futures — Last Trading Day (Contract Roll) — Actor: ICE Futures Europe
  The May 2026 front-month ICE Brent contract expires; the market rolls into June 2026. In a deeply backwardated structure — Brent prompt at ~$112/bbl versus deferred below $90 — the roll compresses the front-month premium and is accompanied by large position liquidation flows. Any weekend developments related to Iran-U.S. negotiations would be reflected in the opening June contract; failure of the April 6 deadline would widen backwardation further and set a new elevated front-month level.

- [2026-03-31, flash estimate] Eurozone HICP Flash Estimate, March 2026 — Actor: Eurostat
  Flash headline and core inflation reading for the euro area in March. Oil-driven energy inflation is a primary input given Brent has risen ~50% YTD; a sharp upside surprise could increase pressure on the ECB to hold rates higher for longer, weighing on European growth expectations and indirectly reducing near-term oil demand projections. The direct price transmission to Brent is through EUR/USD movements and changes in European manufacturing activity expectations.

- [2026-04-01, 10:00 ET] ISM Manufacturing PMI, March 2026 — Actor: Institute for Supply Management
  Monthly gauge of U.S. manufacturing activity and by extension industrial energy demand. Prior February reading was 49.8 (contraction); consensus is approximately 49.5–51.8. A further decline deeper into contraction would reinforce demand-destruction narratives and weigh on crude consumption expectations, potentially capping Brent upside; a recovery above 50 would partially offset demand-side bearish pressure and support prices at elevated levels.

- [2026-04-01, 10:30 ET] EIA Weekly Petroleum Status Report — Week Ending March 27, 2026 — Actor: U.S. Energy Information Administration
  The first EIA inventory data release covering the period after the March 26 diplomatic developments and Trump's April 6 deadline extension. The prior week (ending March 20) showed a massive +6.926 million barrel crude build versus +0.5 million expected — the fifth consecutive build — with Cushing rising 3.421 million barrels. Consensus for the new report is likely a build of similar magnitude given continued IEA reserve deliveries and sanctions waivers. A continued large build would cap Brent upside; a surprise draw would confirm persistent physical tightness and be sharply bullish given markets are primed for supply anxiety.

- [2026-04-02, 08:15 ET] ADP National Employment Report, March 2026 — Actor: ADP / Moody's Analytics
  Private sector employment data serving as a precursor to the March NFP report (delayed to April 7 due to Good Friday). Prior February ADP showed significant weakness; Fed Chair Powell noted job creation had "slowed to essentially zero." A weak print combined with oil at $112/bbl would intensify recession fears and activate the demand-destruction narrative, providing bearish pressure on crude; a stronger-than-expected print would partially offset that risk.

- [2026-04-03] Good Friday — ICE Brent and NYMEX Market Holiday — Actor: ICE Futures Europe / CME Group
  ICE Futures Europe (Brent) and NYMEX (WTI) will be closed for Good Friday on April 3. Reduced liquidity in the days leading up to the holiday can amplify intraday volatility, and any weekend or Sunday-night developments related to Iran-U.S. negotiations will not be priced until Monday April 7 — three hours after Trump's April 6 at 20:00 ET deadline. This timing gap creates significant gap-risk: markets cannot respond to the April 6 deadline outcome in real time, meaning the Monday April 7 open will be the first pricing event post-deadline.

- [2026-04-05] OPEC+ Eight-Country Ministerial Review Meeting — Actor: OPEC+ (Saudi Arabia, Russia, Iraq, UAE, Kuwait, Kazakhstan, Algeria, Oman)
  The eight voluntary-cut OPEC+ producers meet to review market conditions and decide on May 2026 output levels. The April paper increase of +206,000 b/d (agreed March 1) was largely undeliverable given Hormuz constraints on Gulf producers. Options range from suspending May increases (bullish — already priced by several banks) to accelerating the unwind (bearish — unlikely given the supply shock). Any decision to pause or cut the unwind in response to the Hormuz crisis would be sharply bullish; an acceleration would provide a modest bearish signal. The meeting occurs one day before the Trump-Iran April 6 deadline, making the OPEC+ decision a direct input into how Iran interprets market conditions.

- [2026-04-06, 20:00 ET] Trump Deadline — U.S. Pause on Strikes Against Iranian Energy Infrastructure Expires — Actor: U.S. Government / Iran
  President Trump set April 6 at 20:00 ET as the deadline for Iran to make sufficient concessions on reopening the Strait of Hormuz, or face resumed U.S. strikes on Iranian power plants and energy infrastructure. This is the single highest-impact binary price catalyst for Brent within the 4h–7d window. A U.S. decision to strike would remove an additional tranche of Iranian production and infrastructure and likely send Brent above $120/bbl within hours; a deal, formal extension, or ceasefire announcement would compress the estimated $14–18/bbl risk premium and could send Brent down $10–15 within one session. The 15-point U.S. peace proposal remains on the table; Iran's position as of March 29 is to negotiate only through intermediaries.
