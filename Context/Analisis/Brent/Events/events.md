## Events: Brent Crude Oil
Scope: scheduled catalysts, horizon 0-7d; events with confirmed date 2026-03-27 to 2026-04-03

---

- [2026-03-27, 08:30 ET] PCE Price Index and Personal Income/Outlays (February 2026) — Actor: U.S. Bureau of Economic Analysis (BEA)
  February core PCE, the Fed's preferred inflation gauge, with January printing +3.1% YoY (well above the 2.0% target). Consensus expects core PCE to remain elevated near 3.0-3.1% given energy price pass-through. A hotter-than-expected print reinforces higher-for-longer rates, strengthens the USD, and creates demand-side headwinds for Brent; a softer print would weaken the dollar and support risk appetite and oil prices.

- [2026-03-31, end of ICE session] ICE Brent Crude May 2026 Futures — Last Trading Day — Actor: ICE Futures Europe
  The May 2026 front-month ICE Brent contract expires; the roll into the June contract accelerates during the final days. In a backwardated market with elevated near-term premiums, position liquidation and roll-related flows can amplify intraday volatility. The prompt May-June spread will reprice based on the outcome of diplomatic developments and any new supply signals; a failed deal would widen backwardation, while a ceasefire would compress it.

- [2026-03-31 TBD] Eurozone HICP Flash Estimate (March 2026) — Actor: Eurostat
  Flash inflation reading for the euro area. Oil-driven energy inflation is a key input. A sharp rise in headline HICP could increase pressure on the ECB to maintain restrictive policy, which would weigh on European growth expectations and indirectly reduce oil demand forecasts. The direct transmission to Brent is through demand expectations and EUR/USD dynamics.

- [2026-04-01, 10:00 ET] ISM Manufacturing PMI (March 2026) — Actor: Institute for Supply Management
  Manufacturing PMI reflects industrial activity and energy demand. Prior: 49.8 (contraction). Consensus: ~49.5-51.8. A further decline deeper into contraction would signal worsening industrial demand and weigh on crude consumption expectations; a recovery above 50 would partially offset demand-side bearish concerns and support Brent at current levels.

- [2026-04-01, 10:30 ET] EIA Weekly Petroleum Status Report (week ending Mar 27) — Actor: U.S. Energy Information Administration
  The first EIA inventory data release capturing the period after the March 26 diplomatic developments. Prior week showed a massive +6.926 million barrel build (vs. +0.5 million expected), the fifth consecutive build, with Cushing stocks rising 3.421 million barrels. Continued large builds would cap Brent upside; a surprise draw would confirm persistent supply tightness despite IEA reserve releases and sanctions waivers.

- [2026-04-02, 08:15 ET] ADP National Employment Report (March 2026) — Actor: ADP / Moody's Analytics
  Private sector employment data as a precursor to NFP (delayed to April 7 due to Good Friday). Prior February release showed significant weakness; Fed Chair Powell acknowledged job creation had "slowed to essentially zero." Weak data combined with elevated oil prices could increase recession fears and weigh on demand-side expectations for crude.

- [2026-04-03] Good Friday — Market Holiday — Actor: ICE / CME / ECB
  ICE Futures Europe (Brent) and NYMEX (WTI) will be closed for Good Friday on April 3. Reduced liquidity in the days leading up to the holiday could amplify intraday volatility. Any weekend developments related to Iran-U.S. negotiations would not be priced until Monday April 7, creating significant gap risk heading into the April 6 Trump deadline.

- [2026-04-05] OPEC+ Eight-Country Ministerial Meeting — Actor: OPEC+ (Saudi Arabia, Russia, Iraq, UAE, Kuwait, Kazakhstan, Algeria, Oman)
  The eight voluntary-cut OPEC+ countries meet to review market conditions and the April 2026 production adjustment of +206,000 bpd (agreed March 1). Given the Hormuz crisis cutting effective Gulf production by over 10 million bpd, the increase is physically undeliverable for most members. Any decision to suspend the increase would be sharply bullish; confirmation as planned provides mild bearish pressure. The JMMC has confirmed no plans to accelerate output increases before Q3 2026.

- [2026-04-06, 20:00 ET] Trump Deadline for Iran to Reopen Strait of Hormuz — Actor: U.S. Government / Iran
  President Trump extended the pause on U.S. strikes against Iranian energy infrastructure to April 6 at 8 P.M. Eastern Time. This is the single highest-impact binary catalyst for Brent in the next 10 days. If Iran fails to make sufficient concessions on reopening the Strait, the U.S. has threatened to strike Iranian power plants and energy facilities. A strike would likely send Brent above $120; a deal or further extension would compress the $14-18/bbl risk premium. The 15-point U.S. peace proposal remains on the table. Markets will be closed on Good Friday April 3, creating a gap risk as the deadline falls on a Sunday evening.
