## Events: Brent Crude Oil
Scope: scheduled catalysts, horizon 0–7d; events with confirmed date this calendar week

---

- [Mon Mar 23 2026, ongoing — ET] US–Iran Hormuz Strait Negotiation Window (Active) — Actor: White House / Trump Administration
  Trump extended by five days (from Mon Mar 23 late-evening ET) his ultimatum for Iran to fully reopen the Strait of Hormuz, citing "very good and productive" talks via envoys Witkoff and Kushner; the new implied deadline is approximately Saturday Mar 28 late evening ET. The Strait has been closed since Feb 28, disrupting an estimated 20 mb/d of crude and LNG flows — the largest supply disruption in IEA history. If Iran agrees to a reopening framework before the deadline, Brent faces sharp downside from crisis-elevated levels; if talks collapse and the US resumes or executes threatened strikes on Iranian power plants, Brent would spike higher with additional supply-disruption premium.

- [Tue Mar 24 2026, ~09:45 ET] S&P Global Flash PMI — US Manufacturing & Services (March 2026) — Actor: S&P Global
  S&P will release flash PMI estimates for the US; the prior US Manufacturing PMI printed 49.8 (below the 50 contraction threshold, vs. the 51.6–51.8 market estimate), while the Eurozone Manufacturing PMI prior was 47.6. These readings directly proxy near-term industrial activity and refined-product demand. A further decline into contraction, especially in the US, would signal weakening oil demand and exert downward pressure on Brent; a beat above 50 would support the demand outlook and partially offset supply-side geopolitical premium.

- [Tue Mar 24 2026, ~21:30 ET] API Weekly Crude Oil Inventory Report — Actor: American Petroleum Institute
  The API report will cover the week ending Mar 20, released Tuesday evening to the industry. The prior EIA report (week ending Mar 13) showed a +6.2 mb crude build — sharply above the near-zero consensus — partly reflecting IEA strategic reserve release volumes flowing into the US system. The market will watch whether commercial inventories continue building (indicating supply is catching up to the deficit, bearish Brent) or a draw emerges (indicating the 8 mb/d physical deficit is still not being bridged, bullish Brent). The API print typically sets directional tone for the Wednesday EIA release.

- [Wed Mar 25 2026, 10:30 ET] EIA Weekly Petroleum Status Report — Actor: US Energy Information Administration
  The definitive weekly US petroleum inventory data covering crude, gasoline, distillates, and SPR levels for the week ending Mar 20; prior release showed crude +6.2 mb vs. ~+32k consensus. Given the IEA's 400 mb collective stockpile release (US contribution 172 mb, Americas/Europe tranche to begin by end of March) and the ongoing Hormuz blockade cutting Gulf supply, the EIA data will clarify whether SPR drawdown and re-routing of Russian tanker oil (covered by the 30-day US sanctions waiver, expires Apr 11) is offsetting the physical deficit. An unexpected crude draw would confirm market tightness and push Brent higher; a large build would signal the supply bridge is holding and could cap the crisis premium.

- [Thu Mar 26 2026, 18:00 CT / 19:00 ET] Fed Vice Chair Philip Jefferson — "Global Perspectives" Moderated Conversation — Actor: Federal Reserve / Federal Reserve Bank of Dallas
  Vice Chair Jefferson participates in a live moderated Q&A with Dallas Fed President Lorie Logan at the Federal Reserve Bank of Dallas; the March 17–18 FOMC meeting held rates unchanged with Q4 2025 GDP revised to +0.7% annualized and January core PCE at 3.1% YoY. Jefferson's remarks on the economic outlook and the inflationary impact of the energy shock (oil above $100/bbl) are closely watched for signals on the rate-cut timeline. A hawkish tone emphasizing energy-driven inflation persistence would strengthen the dollar and cap Brent upside; a dovish lean acknowledging demand weakness from the economic slowdown would weaken the dollar and provide additional lift to Brent.

- [Fri Mar 27 2026, 08:30 ET] Q4 2025 GDP Final Estimate (Third/Final) — Actor: US Bureau of Economic Analysis (BEA)
  The BEA releases its final Q4 2025 GDP estimate; the second estimate (released Mar 13) came in at +0.7% annualized, well below the +1.5% Dow Jones consensus and down from +4.4% in Q3 2025, driven by the Oct–Nov government shutdown and slowing consumer spending. A further downward revision would deepen demand-destruction concerns for crude and weigh on Brent; an upward revision would partially restore confidence in US economic activity and oil demand. The accompanying corporate profits data and consumer spending breakdown will also be scrutinized for forward demand signals.

- [Fri Mar 27 2026, 08:30 ET] Personal Income & Outlays / PCE Price Index (February 2026) — Actor: US Bureau of Economic Analysis (BEA)
  February 2026 PCE price index and consumer spending data; January core PCE printed +3.1% YoY (well above the Fed's 2.0% target) with PCE headline +2.8% and personal spending +0.4% MoM. This is the Fed's preferred inflation gauge and is particularly critical given the energy-shock transmission from the Hormuz closure. A hotter-than-expected core PCE reading would reinforce a delayed rate-cut path, keep the dollar elevated, and suppress Brent's non-geopolitical demand premium; a softer print would support risk appetite, weaken the dollar, and amplify Brent's upside in the context of the ongoing supply shock.

- [Sat Mar 28 2026, ~late evening ET] Trump–Iran Hormuz Ultimatum Hard Deadline — Actor: White House / Trump Administration
  The five-day extension of Trump's ultimatum for Iran to fully reopen the Strait of Hormuz expires Saturday late evening ET; Trump has signaled he will resume threats to strike Iranian power plants if talks fail, while Iran has not publicly acknowledged direct negotiations. This is the single most binary near-term catalyst for Brent: a confirmed deal or partial Strait reopening would likely trigger a multi-dollar selloff in Brent as the crisis premium partially unwinds; a breakdown leading to additional US strikes on Iran would send Brent sharply higher and risk a second wave of supply disruption on top of the existing 8 mb/d deficit.

- [Tue Mar 31 2026, end of session] ICE Brent Crude Oil Futures — May 2026 Contract Last Trading Day — Actor: ICE Futures Europe
  The May 2026 front-month ICE Brent futures contract (symbol BK May26) expires at the end of the ICE Futures Europe trading session on March 31, with the Last Delivery Day on April 1; the open interest roll from May into June accelerates in the final days of the prior week (Mar 27–28) and into Mon Mar 31. In the context of elevated Brent backwardation driven by the Hormuz supply shock, the roll dynamic can amplify intraday volatility: position liquidation by non-commercial longs near expiry can temporarily suppress spot prices, while the prompt spread (May–June) will reprice as the physical supply outlook evolves with the Mar 28 diplomatic deadline outcome.
