## Impact Analysis: Ibex 35
Context bias: conflicted
Valid window: 3–5 days (through Apr 17 MEFF expiry)

### Narrative 1: ECB Higher-for-Longer Risks Pressuring Rate-Sensitive Sectors
Impact: Favored
Assessment: The dominant conflicted bias is structurally anchored to this narrative: the ECB higher-for-longer dynamic is precisely the mechanism producing the internal divergence between financial heavyweights and rate-sensitive sectors. The Apr 14-15 ECB speaker sequence (Lagarde, Schnabel, de Guindos) serves as the primary near-term catalyst that can either confirm or modulate the NIM tailwind for Santander and BBVA while extending valuation compression pressure on Iberdrola and Endesa. Of the four narratives, this one most directly defines the conflicted bias and carries the highest probability of a clarifying impulse within the valid window.

### Narrative 2: US Trade Policy Escalation Risk for Ibex 35 Exporters
Impact: Opposed
Assessment: This narrative introduces a unidirectional downside vector that works against any constructive resolution of the conflicted bias, as USTR Section 301 confirmation on Apr 15 would crystallize the asymmetric tail risk rather than leave it in a probability distribution. The 2019-2021 Airbus tariff precedent points to sector-specific equity declines of 5-15%, concentrated in names (Inditex, IAG, Ferrovial, Amadeus, Grifols) that individually represent meaningful Ibex 35 float and international revenue exposure. A confirmed escalation would shift the dominant bias from conflicted toward clearly bearish on the export cluster, narrowing the intra-index divergence that currently sustains the conflicted characterization.

### Narrative 3: Spanish Banking Sector Earnings Season
Impact: Favored
Assessment: This narrative is directionally aligned with the conflicted bias in that Bankinter's Apr 15 pre-market print functions as the first empirical test of whether the NIM tailwind thesis embedded in the conflicted rationale is already flowing through to reported earnings. A result at or above consensus (€281m, +4.1% YoY) would reinforce the favored side of the internal split — financials versus rate-sensitives — without resolving the index-level tension, effectively deepening the conflicted character of the bias. The sequencing risk from ECB communication on Apr 14 before the Bankinter open is the key contingency: hawkish ECB commentary could amplify the positive read-across, while dovish signals would complicate the interpretation of any earnings beat.

### Narrative 4: Brent Oil Dislocation and Energy Sector Repricing
Impact: Neutral
Assessment: The Brent dislocation introduces a third fault line (energy repricing) that runs orthogonally to the primary axes of the conflicted bias (ECB rate path vs. trade policy), neither amplifying nor resolving the dominant tension. Repsol's -5.76% move on Apr 13 has already partially repriced the ceasefire scenario, and the reversal risk from a fragile US-Iran ceasefire is real but not directly linked to the ECB or tariff catalysts driving the Apr 14-15 window. The demand-side uncertainty from potential tariff escalation and IMF growth downgrades creates an indirect coupling to Narrative 2, but the energy sector's low Ibex 35 weight relative to financials limits its capacity to alter the aggregate bias direction.

## Aggregate Narrative Balance
The narrative balance amplifies rather than dampens the conflicted dominant bias: Narratives 1 and 3 reinforce the internal divergence between financials and rate-sensitive sectors, Narrative 2 loads asymmetric downside onto the export cluster without a corresponding offsetting force, and Narrative 4 adds a contained but live reversal risk, collectively widening the dispersion of plausible index outcomes across the Apr 14-17 catalyst sequence.
