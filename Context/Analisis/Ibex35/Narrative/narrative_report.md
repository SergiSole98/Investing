### Narrative 1: ECB Higher-for-Longer Risks Pressuring Rate-Sensitive Sectors
Strength: strong
Supporting sources:
- ECB Lagarde Keynote (Washington/IMF, Apr 14, Composite 27) — ECB deposit rate at 2.50%; hawkish signal delays rate cut timeline
- ECB Schnabel — Deglobalization Panel (Apr 14, Composite 24) — most hawkish ECB voice; tariff-driven structural inflation framing provides cover for higher-for-longer

Intentionality risk: low — all central bank communications and statistical releases are independent institutional sources
Summary: A concentrated sequence of ECB speakers (Lagarde, Schnabel, de Guindos) between Apr 14–15, followed by eurozone and Spanish CPI confirmations on Apr 16–17, creates a dense information window that could harden higher-for-longer expectations. Spanish CPI at 3.3% and eurozone HICP at 2.5% (revised upward from 1.9%) reduce the ECB's room to cut, directly pressuring Spanish utilities (Iberdrola, Endesa), REITs, and re-rating the net interest margin outlook for domestic banks. The cumulative weight of these releases makes a dovish pivot in this window unlikely.

Summary: The Apr 15 USTR Section 301 comments deadline is a hard regulatory catalyst: escalation would likely reprise the 2019–2021 Airbus-style tariff episode, which produced 25% levies and 5–15% equity declines in directly affected names. The IMF Spring Meetings running simultaneously (Apr 14–18) amplify the signal through potential downward GDP revisions for the Eurozone and G7-level trade rhetoric. Ibex 35 names with high US revenue exposure — Inditex, Ferrovial, Amadeus, IAG, Grifols — carry asymmetric downside risk in an escalation scenario.

Summary: Bankinter's Apr 15 print is the first live data point for Spanish bank earnings quality under the current rate environment and acts as a sentiment anchor for the entire sector ahead of Santander (Apr 29) and BBVA (Apr 30). A beat against the €281m consensus would likely trigger a broad sector rally given Santander's 9% Ibex 35 index weight; a miss would drag the index disproportionately. The ECB communications on Apr 14 — occurring the evening before Bankinter reports — create a sequencing risk where rate-path repricing lands in the market hours before results, potentially compressing or amplifying the earnings reaction.

The IMF/WB meetings and USTR deadline running simultaneously introduce demand-side variables (growth downgrades, tariff escalation) that could compound or offset the supply-side oil signal.

## Key Tension

The central contradiction is between the ECB higher-for-longer narrative (Narrative 1) and the Spanish banking earnings catalyst (Narrative 3): prolonged elevated rates are simultaneously a net interest margin tailwind for banks and a valuation headwind for the broader index through utilities and REITs, meaning the same ECB signal produces opposite directional pressure on different Ibex 35 sectors. This tension is likely to be resolved — or violently repriced — within a 72-hour window (Apr 14–17) when ECB speakers, Bankinter earnings, Eurostat HICP, Spain CPI, and MEFF monthly expiry all converge; a hawkish Lagarde/Schnabel followed by a Bankinter beat would produce sharp intra-index rotation rather than a clean directional move, while a dovish pivot combined with a Bankinter miss would create broad-based selling pressure concentrated in financials and rate-sensitive names simultaneously.
