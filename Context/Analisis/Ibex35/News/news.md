- [2026-04-08] Fragile US-Iran Ceasefire Announced — Oil Prices Crash ~15%, Market Relief Rally — Source: CNBC
  President Trump announced a two-week ceasefire with Iran on April 7-8, contingent on the "complete, immediate, and safe opening of the Strait of Hormuz," through which ~20% of global oil flows pass. Brent crude plunged approximately 15% to ~$92.28/barrel — the largest single-day decline since the COVID-19 crash — triggering a broad risk rally across global equities, including Spanish banks and energy utilities. Analysts at the Economist Intelligence Unit warned the ceasefire is fragile: "a pause in the conflict, rather than any kind of lasting resolution," with physical damage to regional LNG and refining infrastructure expected to take months to repair.
  Recency: 5 days ago

- [2026-04-07] Repsol Posts Mixed Q1 2026 Provisional Trading Statement — Source: Repsol (TipRanks coverage)
  Repsol's provisional Q1 2026 trading update showed stable overall hydrocarbon production at 539 thousand barrels of oil equivalent per day (kboepd), though consolidated volumes declined in North and Latin America partly due to a third-party pipeline incident in Peru that triggered a national gas supply emergency. Higher Brent and WTI prices versus late 2025, and stronger refining margins year-on-year, provided support, but lower utilization rates at Spanish refineries and weaker chemical margins created a mixed picture. Full Q1 2026 results are scheduled for late April/early May. Repsol's price target was raised to €22.50 (JPMorgan) and to €29 (Berenberg) on April 2.
  Recency: 6 days ago

- [2026-04-07] Santander Share Buyback Progress: 53.8% of Programme Completed — Source: Santander SEC Filing (stocktitan / Santander IR)

Between March 26 and April 7, 2026, Santander repurchased 31.96 million shares at weighted average prices around €9.40. Additionally, a final cash dividend of €0.125 per share (12.5 euro cents) against 2025 profit was confirmed, payable May 5, 2026, bringing total 2025 cash dividends to €0.24 per share (+14% year-on-year). These active capital return signals reinforce bullish sentiment on the stock.
  Recency: 6 days ago

- [2026-04-01] Grifols Completes Full Refinancing of 2027 Maturities via Upsized €3bn Term Loan B — Source: Grifols Press Release / Bloomberg
  Grifols closed a significantly upsized Senior Secured Term Loan B of approximately €3 billion with a seven-year maturity at tighter pricing than at launch, fully refinancing all 2027 debt maturities. The company also doubled its Revolving Credit Facility to $2 billion. Together with the April 13 bond redemption announcement, Grifols now has no material maturities until October 2028, a pivotal deleveraging milestone for a company that has faced persistent liquidity concerns. Bloomberg had earlier (February 2025) reported the CFO's intention to return to public debt markets; that strategy is now fully executed.
