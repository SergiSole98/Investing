## Events: Ibex 35
Scope: scheduled catalysts, horizon 0–7d; events with confirmed date 2026-04-13 to 2026-04-20

- [2026-04-14 14:30 CEST] ECB Vice-President de Guindos — Remarks on monetary policy and financial stability — Actor: ECB / Luis de Guindos
  De Guindos is scheduled to speak at the Cámara de Comercio de Soria event titled "Estado de la política monetaria y de estabilidad financiera en la zona euro." The ECB held rates unchanged at its March meeting, with the deposit rate at 2.50%; markets will parse any signal on whether the May or June Governing Council could move toward a further cut given the March 2026 inflation pick-up to 2.5% y/y. Hawkish language (inflation too persistent) would pressure rate-sensitive Ibex 35 financials and utilities; dovish language (growth risks dominate) would support banks' earnings outlook and real-estate-exposed names.

- [2026-04-14 21:30 CET] ECB President Lagarde — Keynote speech at "The Economic Magic of Equal Opportunity for Women" conference — Actor: ECB / Christine Lagarde (Washington, DC)
  Lagarde speaks in Washington on the sidelines of the IMF/World Bank Spring Meetings; any deviation from the post-March holding pattern on rates or commentary about Middle East-driven energy inflation versus growth headwinds will be watched closely. The ECB's baseline for 2026 projects 0.9% GDP growth and 2.6% average HICP; a more hawkish tilt than expected would weigh on Ibex 35 rate-sensitive sectors (utilities, real estate REITs), while a dovish signal would benefit financials anticipating a steeper rate-cut path. A fireside chat at the Bretton Woods Committee Spring Summit follows at 23:00 CET on the same evening.

- [2026-04-14 22:00 CET] ECB Executive Board Member Schnabel — Panel "Deglobalization and Fragmentation: Mid-Term Challenges for Central Banks" — Actor: ECB / Isabel Schnabel (Washington, DC; IIF Global Outlook Forum)
  Schnabel is among the ECB's most inflation-cautious voices; her statements on how trade fragmentation and tariff escalation affect the eurozone inflation outlook carry significant signal value ahead of the April 30 rate decision. Consensus sees the ECB holding at 2.50% on April 30, but any Schnabel comment flagging upside inflation risk from the US 10% tariff regime or Middle East supply shocks could dampen expectations of a Q2 cut. This would negatively affect Ibex 35 sectors with high leverage (construction, utilities) while banks could see mixed reaction depending on rate-path implications.

- [2026-04-15 —] US USTR Section 301 Written Comments Deadline — Actor: US Trade Representative (USTR)
  April 15, 2026 is the deadline for public comments on the USTR's March 2026 Section 301 investigations covering 16 trade partners including the European Union, targeting alleged industrial overcapacity and forced labour failures. Spain currently faces a 10% US tariff (Section 122, effective February 24, 2026), and a negative post-comment signal raising the risk of additional Section 301 duties would weigh on Ibex 35 exporters with US exposure (Inditex, Grifols, Ferrovial, Amadeus IT, IAG). A de-escalation signal or constructive post-comment framing would be a relief for those names. Public hearings are scheduled from April 28 onward.

- [2026-04-15 ~09:00 CET] Bankinter Q1 2026 Earnings — Actor: Bankinter (BKT.MC)
  Bankinter is expected to publish Q1 2026 results around mid-April (the bank typically reports quarterly results in the third week of January, April, July, and October). Analyst consensus (JB Capital) estimates net profit of approximately €281 million, up ~4.1% year-on-year, supported by net interest income growth of +5.6% and fee income growth of +7.7%. A beat on NII or credit quality would be positive for Bankinter shares and would provide a read-across for other Spanish banks reporting later in the month (Santander April 29, BBVA April 30); a miss or downward guidance revision on NIM compression would weigh on the broader Ibex 35 financial sub-index.

- [2026-04-16 10:00 CET] Eurozone Final HICP Inflation (March 2026) — Actor: Eurostat
  Eurostat publishes the final harmonised consumer price index for the euro area for March 2026; the flash estimate showed headline HICP at +2.5% y/y (up from 1.9% in February), driven by a sharp 4.9% annual rise in energy prices linked to Middle East supply tension. Consensus expects the final print to confirm the flash estimate; a downward revision would revive ECB cut expectations and support rate-sensitive Ibex 35 sectors (utilities, real estate), while an upward revision to core HICP would delay anticipated ECB easing and pressure bond proxies while modestly benefiting banks. The release also provides country-level final data for Spain.

- [2026-04-17 ~10:00 CET] MEFF Ibex 35 Monthly Futures and Options Expiry — Actor: MEFF / BME Clearing
  The third Friday of April is the standard monthly settlement date for MEFF Ibex 35 futures and options contracts. Settlement is based on the opening auction price of Ibex 35 constituent stocks on expiry day; elevated open interest near current index levels (around 12,000–13,000) typically produces increased intraday volatility and above-average volume in the underlying stocks and ETFs in the final hour before and at the open. Net positioning unwinding can amplify any directional move driven by concurrent macro catalysts already in the window (HICP confirmation, ECB speakers, US tariff commentary).

- [2026-04-17 (week of) ~] Spain INE CPI Final (March 2026) — Actor: Instituto Nacional de Estadística (INE)
  INE is expected to release the final March 2026 Spanish CPI around mid-April following the flash estimate published on March 27, which showed annual CPI at 3.3% y/y — the highest reading since June 2024 — versus 2.9% in February. Core CPI and the HICP-equivalent (IPCA) components will be scrutinised for stickiness signals. A confirmation of 3.3% or higher would reinforce the narrative that Spanish inflation is running materially above the eurozone average, potentially complicating the ECB's easing path and weighing on Spanish utility and infrastructure names with regulated tariff exposure; a downside surprise would provide relief.

- [2026-04-14 to 2026-04-18 (ongoing)] IMF / World Bank Spring Meetings — Actor: IMF, World Bank, G7/G20 Finance Ministers
  The IMF and World Bank Spring Meetings take place in Washington, DC during the week of April 13–18, 2026, with finance ministers and central bank governors including Lagarde and Schnabel in attendance. The IMF's World Economic Outlook update will be published in this window, with eurozone and Spanish GDP growth revisions expected given tariff escalation and Middle East supply shocks. Negative IMF GDP revisions for the eurozone (currently 0.9% for 2026) or explicit warnings about trade war escalation would weigh on export-oriented Ibex 35 components (IAG, Amadeus, Ferrovial, Inditex); positive risk sentiment from a US-EU trade-deal progress signal would benefit the same names.
