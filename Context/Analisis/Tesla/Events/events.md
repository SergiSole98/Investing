## Events: Tesla (TSLA)
Scope: scheduled catalysts, horizon 0–14d; events with confirmed or expected date April 9–22, 2026
Reference date: 2026-04-09 | Spot: ~$343.25

- [2026-04-09 (today)] Trump Reciprocal Tariffs Take Effect (+34% China, +20% EU, +24% Japan) — Actor: US Government / China
  The tariff package announced April 2 ("Liberation Day") takes effect today. China already retaliated with 125% counter-tariffs on US goods. Tesla has disclosed in SEC filings that it is "exposed to retaliatory tariffs on exports" — Model S and X sales in China have already ceased. The implementation day itself creates market volatility that suppresses TSLA alongside the broader market. Each subsequent escalation or de-escalation between the US and China on trade policy is a live micro-catalyst for Tesla's China segment exposure and the broader macro multiple. A 90-day tariff pause announcement at any point before April 22 would be the single largest positive macro catalyst for TSLA in the near term.

- [~2026-04-10] Expected EU FSD Regulatory Approval (UN R-171 Standard) — Actor: European Commission / UNECE
  Tesla guided that EU regulatory approval for FSD under the UN R-171 standard was expected around April 10. Approval would unlock FSD deployment across all EU member states by summer 2026, opening a software revenue stream (FSD subscription ~€199/month or €6,900–€15,000 one-time) in a market representing ~15–20% of Tesla's addressable base. The approval date is management-guided, not independently confirmed by EU authorities. If confirmed, the near-term stock impact is likely 2–5% positive — significant but secondary to the April 22 earnings binary. A regulatory delay or rejection would be a mild negative catalyst and would weaken the technology-optionality narrative. Monitor EU regulatory announcements from UNECE or national transport ministries on April 10.

- [2026-04-22 17:30 ET] Q1 2026 Earnings Call — Actor: Tesla management / Wall Street
  Q1 2026 earnings and the subsequent conference call are the single highest-impact scheduled event in the 13-day window. Consensus EPS: $0.24 diluted (vs. $0.15 year-ago). Critical watch items:
  (1) Automotive gross margin: consensus ~17–18%; a print below 16% triggers a sell; above 19% triggers a buy.
  (2) Energy storage recovery from 8.8 GWh Q1 miss — does management guide to Q2 recovery?
  (3) Tariff mitigation strategy — how does management address the April 9 tariff implementation?
  (4) Cybercab production update — confirmed start, unit economics, cost per vehicle.
  (5) Q2 2026 delivery guidance — is the 50,363-unit inventory cleared or are further price cuts needed?
  (6) Optimus Gen 3 production status at Fremont.
  The earnings call is a binary event: the stock historically moves 8–12% in the two sessions following the release. Low current IV rank (~20) means the options market is not pricing an unusually large move, creating a potential asymmetric setup for options buyers who expect realized volatility to exceed implied volatility around the event.

- [2026-04-09 (ongoing)] NHTSA FSD Engineering Analysis — Resolution Pending (6–18 months) — Actor: NHTSA
  The Engineering Analysis (upgraded from Preliminary Evaluation on March 19) covering 3.2 million vehicles remains open with no scheduled resolution date within the valid window. However, any NHTSA communication (public statement, Tesla response filing, preliminary finding report) within the April 9–22 window would be a catalyst. A preliminary finding citing a defect would accelerate the recall timeline and pressure the stock -5% to -10%; a public statement indicating the investigation is trending toward software remedy would reduce tail risk and provide modest relief. Monitor NHTSA's public docket for filings related to investigation numbers EA 26E-018 (FSD visibility) and PE 25-037 (FSD traffic violations). No announcement is expected within the 14-day window based on NHTSA's typical investigation cadence.

- [2026-04 (this month)] Cybercab Production Start at Giga Texas — Actor: Tesla operations
  Tesla confirmed on its Q4 2025 earnings call that Cybercab production would begin at Giga Texas in April 2026. A confirmed production start within the valid window — announced via Musk tweet/post, official press release, or April 22 earnings disclosure — would remove an execution uncertainty and validate the robotaxi commercialization timeline. The absence of a public confirmation before April 22 creates ambiguity that the earnings call must resolve. The primary risks: production equipment installation delays, regulatory hold (requiring NHTSA clearance before deploying new autonomous vehicles), or supply chain disruptions from tariff regime changes. Each city expansion announcement in H1 2026 is a secondary catalyst.

- [2026-04-11 08:30 ET] US Retail Sales (March 2026) — Actor: US Census Bureau
  Retail sales for March 2026 are released April 11. Michigan Consumer Sentiment fell to 53.3 in March amid gasoline price shock and equity volatility — the lowest reading in the data series in recent years — suggesting consumer spending may have weakened materially. A significant miss in retail sales (e.g., -1% or greater MoM) would amplify recession fears and reduce consumer confidence in large-ticket purchases, incrementally bearish for EV demand and thus TSLA. A surprise strength print would reduce recession risk premium in the stock. Impact on TSLA is secondary to the April 22 earnings binary, but a weak print creates an additional headwind narrative in the days preceding the earnings call.

- [2026-04-14 08:30 ET] US PPI (Producer Price Index, March 2026) — Actor: Bureau of Labor Statistics
  PPI for March 2026 is released April 14 — the week before earnings. A hot PPI print driven by tariff pass-through would reinforce the inflationary environment, reduce probability of near-term Fed rate cuts, and maintain pressure on consumer financing costs (currently a structural EV demand headwind). The Fed rate environment directly impacts Tesla sales through auto loan rates; any PPI signal pointing to persistent elevated rates is marginally bearish for TSLA. A cool PPI would revive rate-cut expectations and improve the consumer affordability narrative for EVs.

- [2026-04-11 (Friday, ~15:30 ET)] CFTC COT Report — Institutional Equity Positioning Data — Actor: CFTC / Options Market
  While TSLA is not a futures market in the traditional COT sense, the options market positioning data (put/call ratio, large open interest strikes, dealer hedging flows) will be updated as the IV expansion begins into the April 22 earnings event. The current IV rank of ~20 (low for TSLA historically) is notable: it implies the options market is not pricing an unusually large move. As the April 22 event approaches, IV will expand mechanically, making options more expensive and creating pressure for directional positions to be established or hedged. Monitor the $300 and $400 strike open interest as key levels where dealer gamma hedging will amplify price movements.

- [H1 2026, ongoing] Robotaxi City Expansion Announcements — Actor: Tesla / Local Regulators
  Tesla plans to expand unsupervised robotaxi service to Dallas, Houston, Phoenix, Miami, Orlando, Tampa, and Las Vegas in H1 2026. Each city launch announcement is a potential near-term sentiment catalyst. Regulatory approval is required at the state or municipal level in each city; Texas (no state-level AV approval required) and Florida (favorable AV framework) are likely to proceed more rapidly than California or New York. A high-profile city launch (e.g., Dallas or Miami) before the April 22 earnings call would be a positive catalyst; any regulatory rejection or delay announcement would amplify the technology-execution narrative risk.
