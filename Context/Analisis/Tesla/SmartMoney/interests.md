## Interests Map: Tesla (TSLA)
Scope: structural incentives of identified smart money actors
Reference date: 2026-04-09
Price context: TSLA ~$343.25; Q1 2026 earnings April 22, 2026

---

### ARK Invest / Cathie Wood
Bias: bullish | Confidence: high
Reason: ARK's entire investment thesis and ARKK's AUM (~$7–9B) are disproportionately tied to TSLA performance; TSLA at 9.94% of ARKK is the fund's single largest holding. ARK's published $2,600 2029 target creates a reference frame that makes every price below $600 appear as a deep-value opportunity. ARK's business model depends on maintaining a differentiated ultra-bullish view on disruptive technology — abandoning the Tesla bull thesis would undermine ARK's brand identity. The 3-day $28M purchase (April 6–8) confirms conviction is intact.
Near-term deadline: April 22 Q1 2026 earnings — a strong auto margin recovery with Cybercab production confirmation would validate the near-term bull case and could trigger further ARK accumulation. A margin miss or Cybercab delay would put ARK in the difficult position of defending the thesis publicly while sitting on paper losses.

### Elon Musk (Insider)
Bias: bullish | Confidence: high
Reason: Musk's ~13% equity stake (~$130B+ at current prices) makes him the largest incentivized Tesla bull. His compensation structure ties to revenue and market cap milestones (though the landmark 2018 pay package remains tied up in litigation). Musk controls the strategic roadmap: Cybercab production decisions, FSD architecture, Optimus timeline, and executive hiring are all his calls. His interest in Tesla's stock price is direct and maximal. The DOGE association is the key tension: Musk's short-term political engagement has materially damaged Tesla's commercial brand, but Musk appears to prioritize his political activities over stock price management — a behavioral anomaly for a >$100B stakeholder.
Near-term deadline: DOGE dissolution scheduled for July 4, 2026 — beyond the valid window but a medium-term catalyst for brand rehabilitation. April 22 earnings call is the primary near-term vehicle for Musk to reassert the technology narrative and restore market confidence.

### Short Sellers (aggregate)
Bias: bearish | Confidence: high
Reason: $16.67B in short interest represents active bearish conviction at scale. The short thesis has multiple vectors: (1) demand destruction from tax credit expiry and brand damage; (2) autonomous technology overpromise and regulatory delays (NHTSA Engineering Analysis); (3) valuation compression as competition intensifies and growth normalizes; (4) Musk execution risk (DOGE distraction, SEC litigation exposure). Near-term deadline: April 22 Q1 2026 earnings — a strong beat with Cybercab confirmation could trigger short-covering and add fuel to a post-earnings rally; a miss would invite adding shorts back aggressively.

### NHTSA
Bias: neutral (regulatory mandate) | Confidence: high
Reason: NHTSA's institutional mandate is vehicle safety, not stock price direction. However, its Engineering Analysis represents the most severe regulatory threat to Tesla's autonomous vehicle thesis in the valid window. NHTSA gains nothing from punishing Tesla specifically; it gains from establishing clear safety standards for autonomous vehicles as the industry scales. The incentive is toward a negotiated solution (software OTA remedy) rather than a full recall — consistent with the 2022 Autopilot recall resolution via OTA update. NHTSA's pace is slow by institutional design; no resolution within the 14-day window is the base case.
Near-term deadline: No hard deadline within the April 9–22 window. Tesla must respond to NHTSA's Engineering Analysis with a formal engineering report; the timeline for that response is 30–90 days from investigation upgrade. Monitor NHTSA docket for Tesla submissions.
