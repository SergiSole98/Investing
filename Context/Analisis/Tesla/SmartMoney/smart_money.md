Price-insensitive structural long as long as TSLA remains in S&P 500 and NASDAQ-100. No near-term catalyst for rebalancing at current weight levels.

3. BlackRock — Category: Passive Index Manager / Active ETF | Influence: high

4. Morgan Stanley / Adam Jonas — Category: Investment Bank Research | Influence: high
   Evidence: Morgan Stanley equity research coverage; MarketBeat PT consensus tracker
   Notes: Overweight rating, $410 price target. Jonas is Tesla's most prominent sell-side bull at a major bank; his coverage drives substantial institutional client positioning. Jonas's $410 PT (vs. $343 current) implies ~19% upside and is above consensus. His thesis centers on the AI/robotics platform re-rating: if Cybercab scales and FSD monetizes, the company is worth multiples of current auto-sector comps. He acknowledges near-term margin caution but views execution risk as priced in at $340.

5. Wedbush Securities / Dan Ives — Category: Investment Bank Research | Influence: medium
   Evidence: MarketBeat PT consensus; Teslarati coverage of Wedbush research
   Notes: Outperform rating, $600 price target — the highest among major broker targets, implying 75% upside. Ives characterizes Tesla as a "tech company not a car company" and is the most bullish non-ARK institutional voice. His AI-driven autonomous vehicle platform thesis drives significant retail and institutional sentiment. The $600 target creates a narrative anchor that attracts momentum buyers. Like ARK, Wedbush has maintained bullish calls through prior selloffs.

6. Goldman Sachs — Category: Investment Bank Research | Influence: high
   Evidence: MarketBeat PT consensus; Teslarati "Tesla stock gets hit with shock move from Wall Street analysts"
   Notes: Hold/Neutral rating, $375 price target (cut from $405 following Q1 delivery miss on April 2). Goldman's Hold is effectively a "wait for clarity on margins before adding" signal; their rate-sensitive macro research team also tracks TSLA as a high-beta consumer discretionary in the tariff environment.

7. JPMorgan / Ryan Brinkman — Category: Investment Bank Research | Influence: high
   Evidence: Financial Content "JPMorgan Stands Firm on Bearish Tesla Call," April 6, 2026; MarketBeat PT tracker
   Notes: Underweight rating, $145 price target — most bearish major broker call, implying ~-57% downside from current. The Q1 delivery miss and 50,363-unit inventory build are consistent with his demand-weakness thesis. JPMorgan's persistent bearish stance creates overhead resistance, drives client hedging activity, and anchors the bear-case narrative at $145.

8. HSBC Research — Category: Investment Bank Research | Influence: medium
   Evidence: MarketBeat PT consensus; multiple financial media reports
   Notes: Reduce rating, $119 price target — the most extreme bear case among named major brokers, implying ~-65% downside. HSBC's thesis aligns with JPMorgan's "auto company valuation" framework but is more aggressive in its multiple compression assumption. At $119, HSBC implies Tesla would trade at approximately 8–10x forward earnings on a fully-valued automotive basis. The extreme bear case functions as a tail risk anchor rather than a consensus signal.

9. Elon Musk (insider/executive) — Category: Company Insider | Influence: high
   Evidence: Tesla Q4 2025 earnings call; SEC insider filings; public statements
   Notes: ~13% equity ownership stake (~$130B+ at current prices). Musk controls Tesla's strategic direction: Cybercab timeline, Optimus production, FSD architecture, and executive talent decisions are all personally driven. His dual role as Tesla CEO and DOGE advisor/Trump confidant creates the brand damage narrative that is directly suppressing demand. There is a structural tension between Musk's political activities and Tesla's commercial interests; the resolution of DOGE's dissolution (July 4, 2026 per executive order) may represent a gradual brand rehabilitation catalyst — but that is beyond the current valid window.

10. Short Sellers (aggregate) — Category: Speculative / Bearish | Influence: high
    Evidence: MarketBeat short interest data, updated April 2026
    Notes: $16.67 billion in short interest (largest US stock by dollar value); ~60.86 million shares short (~1.82% of float). Short interest declined marginally in the last reporting period, suggesting shorts are not aggressively adding at current levels (~$340s) — consistent with near-term price support at these levels. Short squeeze risk: at 1.82% float short, a squeeze would require significant news catalyst (e.g., strong Q1 earnings + Cybercab production confirmed). The short interest acts as a structural headwind that limits rallies and amplifies any negative catalyst.

11. NHTSA (National Highway Traffic Safety Administration) — Category: Regulator | Influence: medium

The investigation timeline (6–18 months to resolution) extends beyond the current valid window, but any public NHTSA statement or Tesla response filing could function as a catalyst within the April 9–22 window.
