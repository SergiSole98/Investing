- [2026-04-02] Tesla Q1 2026 deliveries miss estimates: 358,023 vs. 365,645 consensus (-2.1%); stock falls 5%+ — Source: Tesla IR / CNBC / Electrek
  Tesla reported Q1 2026 deliveries of 358,023 vehicles against consensus expectations of approximately 365,645, a miss of ~7,600 units (-2.1%). Production was 408,386 units, creating an inventory build of approximately 50,363 units — the widest production-to-delivery gap in at least four years and a clear signal of demand softness rather than supply disruption. Model 3/Y (341,893 deliveries, 394,611 production) accounted for virtually all of the inventory build. Energy storage deployments also missed sharply at 8.8 GWh vs. ~14.4 GWh expected (-38% QoQ from Q4's record 14.2 GWh), though Tesla attributed this to project timing. The stock fell over 5% on April 2, the steepest single-session drop of 2026 at that point. The delivery miss is the second consecutive quarter below estimates, amplifying concern that demand erosion — driven by EV tax credit expiry (September 30, 2025), Musk brand damage, and high financing costs — is structural rather than cyclical.

- [2026-04-09] Trump reciprocal tariffs take effect — +34% on China, +20% on EU, +24% on Japan — Source: Reuters / Fox Business
  Trump's "Liberation Day" tariff schedule (announced April 2) officially takes effect today (April 9), adding 34% on Chinese imports, 20% on EU imports, and 24% on Japanese imports, on top of the existing 25% tariff on all imported cars and auto parts. China immediately retaliated with 125% tariffs on US goods. Tesla warned in SEC filings it is "exposed to retaliatory tariffs on exports." Practical impacts already visible: Tesla stopped taking new orders for Model S and Model X in China due to the impossibility of importing US-made vehicles profitably. Tesla's mitigation: Giga Shanghai sources 90%+ of components locally, largely insulating the China segment for Model 3/Y. However, specialty components (rare earth magnets, semiconductor inputs) retain China sourcing; cost headwinds are estimated in the high single digits to low double digits per unit. The affordable Model Y US variant planned for Q2 2026 was already delayed to Q3/Q4 2026 due to tariff disruption.

- [2026-04-08] FSD v14.3 (build 2026.2.9.6) rolls out — "better reaction time," rewritten AI compiler with MLIR — Source: Tesla Oracle / Electrek
  Tesla deployed FSD v14.3 on April 8, featuring a rewritten AI compiler using MLIR (machine learning intermediate representation) that Tesla engineers describe as enabling "better reaction time" by improving the computational pipeline for inference. The update is incrementally positive for the autonomy thesis but is not a step-change catalyst on its own; FSD is still supervised and requires a driver behind the wheel in most US states. The significance of this release is in the context of the pending NHTSA Engineering Analysis and the expected EU regulatory approval (~April 10): a demonstrably improving FSD version strengthens Tesla's regulatory argument. The release does not address the core NHTSA probe concern (visibility impairment from sun glare, dust, and fog), which targets a different failure mode.

- [2026-03-19] NHTSA upgrades Tesla FSD visibility investigation to Engineering Analysis; covers 3.2 million vehicles — Source: Electrek / CBT News
  NHTSA escalated its Tesla FSD camera visibility investigation to Engineering Analysis — the final regulatory stage before a mandatory recall can be ordered. The probe covers approximately 3.2 million vehicles and has identified nine accidents, including one fatality, where camera visibility was impaired by sun glare, dust, or fog without generating adequate driver alerts. A separate FSD probe (2.88 million vehicles, 50+ traffic safety violation reports) remains at Preliminary Evaluation stage. Engineering Analysis investigations have a historical progression time of 6–18 months before a recall decision. A mandatory recall on 3.2 million vehicles would be the most damaging near-term regulatory catalyst: it would undermine the core FSD/autonomy commercial narrative at a critical juncture (Cybercab production just beginning), generate significant legal liability, and trigger institutional repositioning among funds that hold TSLA on the autonomy thesis.

- [2026-03-17] Tesla confirmed as buyer in LG Energy Solution's $4.3 billion LFP battery deal for Megapack 3 — Source: Electrek
  The US Department of Energy confirmed Tesla as the buyer in LG Energy Solution's $4.3 billion LFP (lithium iron phosphate) battery supply agreement to support domestic Megapack 3 production. The deal secures US-based battery supply at scale, partially insulating the energy storage business from China-linked supply chain tariff risk. LFP chemistry is lower cost per kWh than NMC alternatives, supporting the margin improvement trajectory in the energy segment (30.3% gross margin for first 9 months of 2025). This is the single most structurally positive news item for the energy thesis in the past 30 days.

- [2026-04-02] Affordable Model Y US variant delayed to Q3/Q4 2026 due to tariff disruption — Source: Reuters / Electrek
  Tesla's plan to launch a lower-priced US-made Model Y variant targeting 250,000 units/year was pushed back from Q2 2026 to Q3/Q4 2026 due to supply chain disruption from the tariff environment. The delay is meaningful because the affordable variant was Tesla's primary strategy to address the top-of-funnel demand problem created by the EV tax credit expiry. A Q3/Q4 launch means demand headwinds persist for at least two more quarters at current pricing levels.
