## News: Brent Crude Oil (ICE Brent)
Scope: price-relevant items, horizon 4h–7d (project); sources within last 7 days

- [Mar 24, 2026] Trump extends Iran ultimatum to Friday, cites "productive" early contacts — Source: Business Standard / Reuters
  President Trump postponed a planned US military strike on Iranian energy infrastructure, granting a 5-day window expiring approximately Friday March 28, 2026. Iran publicly denied any substantive negotiations are underway, calling Trump's claims an attempt to manipulate markets. Brent fell ~11% on Monday (to ~$99) on the pause announcement, then recovered to $104.49 on Tuesday as Iran's denial restored supply risk premium. The outcome of this deadline is the dominant near-term binary for Brent price direction.

- [Mar 24, 2026] Brent recovers to $104.49 after Iran denies US talks — Source: CNBC / Reuters
  Brent crude (May contract) gained ~4.55% on March 24 after Iran's official rejection of Trump's "productive conversations" claim reignited supply disruption fears. The prior session's collapse to $99 had represented the steepest single-day drop since 2020. Market is pricing a ~$10–$15 uncertainty premium above what a deal-scenario price would imply (~$85–$90 range cited by analysts).

- [Mar 24, 2026] XTB Chart of the Day: $95 support holds, backwardation intact — Source: XTB
  Technical analysis published March 24 confirms Brent has defended the $95 level (38.2% Fibonacci retracement from 2026 low) on all intraday tests. The forward curve remains in pronounced backwardation; calendar spreads through year-end are elevated but slightly below the March 12 extreme levels. A $1–2 drop in calendar spreads is flagged as the signal for a definitive trend reversal.

- [Mar 23, 2026] Oil slides as Trump announces 5-day pause on Iran strikes — Source: CNBC
  Brent dropped to approximately $99–$100 after Trump announced he was pausing US military strikes and had "very good and productive conversations" with Iranian officials. The sell-off was triggered by reduced near-term supply disruption probability. However, Israeli strikes on Iranian gas infrastructure continued through the night, and Iran retaliated against Bahrain and UAE targets, keeping the conflict structurally active.

- [Mar 22, 2026] Brent tops $112 as Trump issues 48-hour ultimatum to Iran — Source: AD-HOC News / CNBC
  Brent peaked at $112/bbl on Friday March 22 after Trump threatened to bomb Iranian energy infrastructure within 48 hours unless Iran reopened the Strait of Hormuz. The Strait remains at approximately 5% of its normal ~20 mb/d throughput. This peak now serves as the upper resistance level; a return above $110 would require a material escalation beyond the current conflict level.

- [Mar 18, 2026] Saudi Red Sea exports hit record 3.8 mb/d, bypass capacity approaching maximum — Source: Reuters
  Saudi Aramco's Yanbu loadings hit 3.8 mb/d in March, up from 1.4 mb/d in February. The East-West Pipeline (Petroline) maximum capacity is ~5 mb/d but current Saudi normal Hormuz throughput was ~6 mb/d, implying a structural ~1–1.5 mb/d export deficit that cannot be closed via bypass routes. This structural supply shortfall supports sustained backwardation regardless of near-term diplomatic developments.

- [Mar 16, 2026] IEA: further oil stock releases possible if needed — Source: Reuters / IEA
  IEA executive director stated member countries could release additional strategic reserves beyond the March 11 record 400M barrel authorization if supply conditions warrant. This "open-ended" commitment serves as a price ceiling signal; markets interpret it as capping Brent upside at approximately $110–$120 before another IEA intervention. The current 400M barrel drawdown is expected to take ~120 days, adding ~3.3 mb/d of supply to spot markets during the release window.

- [Mar 15, 2026] Oil prices moderate as some tankers resume Strait of Hormuz transit — Source: Reuters
  Brent pulled back to ~$100.21 on March 15 after Iran allowed a limited number of Indian-flagged vessels to transit the Strait of Hormuz. The partial reopening was not formal and tanker transits remained well below normal levels. The episode demonstrated the market's sensitivity to any incremental change in Strait status: even partial re-openings can trigger sharp $5–$10 downside moves in front-month Brent.
