## Smart Money: Brent Crude Oil (ICE Brent May 2026)
Scope: major institutional actors with structural price influence; public evidence only

1. OPEC+ Core Eight (Saudi Arabia, Russia, Iraq, UAE, Kuwait, Kazakhstan, Algeria, Oman) — Category: national oil company consortium / sovereign producer | Influence: high
   Evidence: OPEC+ press release, March 1, 2026 (opec.org/pr-detail/1619593-1-march-2026.html)
   Notes: Collectively controls ~40% of global crude supply; April 2026 decision to unwind 206,000 bpd of voluntary cuts while retaining flexibility to reverse is the primary supply-side price lever.

2. IEA Member Governments (32 nations, led by US, Japan, EU) — Category: government / strategic reserve authority | Influence: high
   Evidence: IEA collective action announcement, March 11, 2026; IEA update note March 16, 2026 (iea.org)
   Notes: Authorized the largest emergency reserve release in history (400 million barrels); active drawdown creates a direct short-term supply offset to the Hormuz disruption, capping the upside.

3. Saudi Aramco — Category: national oil company (producer / exporter) | Influence: high
   Evidence: Reuters, March 6 and March 18, 2026 — Yanbu loadings data, East-West Pipeline reports
   Notes: Redirecting exports via Petroline (East-West Pipeline) to the Red Sea at record 3.8 mb/d; bypass capacity (~5 mb/d max) versus prior Hormuz throughput (~6 mb/d) leaves a structural 1+ mb/d shortfall, directly supporting backwardation.

4. Hedge Funds / Managed Money — Category: CTA / macro hedge fund | Influence: high
   Evidence: Bloomberg, March 13, 2026; Varchev analysis citing CFTC data through March 2026
   Notes: Shifted from record 351,000 net long lots (6-year high, early March) to ~153,000 lots after six consecutive weekly reductions; behavior consistent with event-driven profit-taking and geopolitical risk de-positioning following Trump's pause announcement.

5. US Strategic Petroleum Reserve / Department of Energy — Category: government / strategic reserve | Influence: high
   Evidence: IEA collective action decision, March 11, 2026; Reuters, March 11, 2026
   Notes: Contributing 172 million barrels to the IEA release; active physical selling into spot markets over the next ~120 days creates a persistent, measurable downward supply-side pressure on Brent spreads.

6. Goldman Sachs / Morgan Stanley / JPMorgan (Swap Dealers) — Category: prime broker / investment bank | Influence: medium
   Evidence: CFTC Disaggregated COT Report — Petroleum, March 10, 2026 (cftc.gov)
   Notes: Swap dealer positioning shows sizable long exposure as market makers for commodity index products; their rolling and rebalancing activity amplifies directional moves at expiry.

7. Saudi Arabia Public Investment Fund (PIF) — Category: sovereign wealth fund | Influence: medium
   Evidence: Reuters, March 6, 2026 — Gulf SWF review of investment strategy
   Notes: Fiscal breakeven ~$80/bbl; structural interest in $80–$100 price range to fund Vision 2030; reviewing investment allocations amid disrupted oil revenue and increased defense expenditures.

8. Abu Dhabi Investment Authority (ADIA) — Category: sovereign wealth fund | Influence: medium
   Evidence: Reuters, March 6, 2026 — Gulf sovereign wealth fund responses
   Notes: ~$1 trillion AUM; ADNOC-linked revenues disrupted; ADIA typically does not trade Brent futures directly but its rebalancing of energy equity and fixed-income allocations can affect correlated markets.

9. Vitol / Trafigura / Glencore (physical commodity traders) — Category: commodity trading house | Influence: medium
   Evidence: Reuters/Bloomberg reporting on Hormuz rerouting trades, March 2026 (publicly cited flow data)
   Notes: As the dominant physical Brent lifters and re-sellers, their basis trading and forward hedging activity directly shapes time spread dynamics; currently managing extreme logistical complexity from tanker rerouting.

10. Kuwait Investment Authority (KIA) — Category: sovereign wealth fund | Influence: medium
    Evidence: Reuters, March 6, 2026 — Gulf SWF review; SWFI Institute fund rankings
    Notes: ~$920 billion AUM; KPC (Kuwait Petroleum Corporation) exports disrupted by Hormuz closure; KIA's drawdown risk is price-negative if it liquidates equity holdings to offset fiscal shortfall.
