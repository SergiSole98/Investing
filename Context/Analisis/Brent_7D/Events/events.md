## Events: Brent Crude Oil (ICE Brent)
Scope: scheduled catalysts, horizon 0–7d; events with confirmed date this calendar week

- [Mar 28, 2026 — approximate, EDT] Trump Iran ultimatum deadline expires — Actor: US Government / Donald Trump
  Trump's extended 5-day pause on US military strikes against Iranian energy infrastructure expires around Friday March 28. Iran has officially denied conducting negotiations, Israel continues airstrikes on Iranian gas infrastructure, and the US continues military deployments (82nd Airborne). If Trump extends the deadline again → Brent likely stabilizes or falls toward $95–$98; if he orders strikes on Iranian energy facilities → Brent likely surges above $112 toward the $130–$150 range cited by analysts; if Iran makes a concession or deal is announced → Brent likely collapses to $85–$90. Polymarket assigns ~51% probability of a ceasefire by April 30; the near-term Friday deadline is the highest-impact scheduled event within the 7-day window.

- [Mar 26, 2026 — Wednesday, ~10:30 AM EDT] EIA Weekly Petroleum Status Report — Actor: US Energy Information Administration
  Weekly US crude oil inventory data including SPR drawdown progress (US pledged 172M barrels over ~120 days) and commercial stock builds/draws. Consensus expectation: continued commercial stock build reflecting weak near-term demand and IEA release inflows. A larger-than-expected inventory build (>3M bbl) would be bearish for Brent; a draw driven by export rerouting or refinery demand would be bullish. Prior week was +2.1M bbl commercial crude build.

- [Mar 25, 2026 — Tuesday, ~4:30 PM EDT] API Weekly Crude Oil Stock Report — Actor: American Petroleum Institute
  Preliminary industry crude stock survey, released one day before the official EIA report. Typically moves WTI and Brent by $0.50–$1.50 on the evening release. Expected to show a build given IEA release deliveries entering the US distribution system.

- [Mar 27–28, 2026] Pakistan-mediated Iran-US back-channel talks (potential) — Actor: Pakistan / Iran / US
  Reuters reported on March 24 that Pakistan has offered to host direct US-Iran peace talks, with discussions at "an advanced stage for a meeting within a week." If a confirmed meeting is announced → likely triggers a significant de-escalation trade in Brent (−$5 to −$10); if Pakistan confirms no deal is imminent → reinforces Iran supply-disruption premium. No confirmed time or venue as of March 24.

- [Mar 25–31, 2026] Goldman Sachs Commodity Roll / Month-End Index Rebalancing — Actor: Commodity index managers (GSCI, BCOM)
  End-of-month commodity index roll period: swap dealers and index-tracking funds mechanically sell front-month (May) Brent contracts and buy second-month (June) contracts. This roll typically creates temporary downward pressure on the May contract spread of ~$0.30–$0.60 per barrel and is a predictable, recurring event. Effect is limited in magnitude but adds mild directional pressure toward calendar spread compression.

- [Mar 31, 2026] OPEC+ April production quota takes effect — Actor: OPEC+ Core Eight
  The agreed 206,000 bpd increase in April production is scheduled to begin. However, with Saudi Aramco and UAE already operating near their bypass pipeline capacity limits, the physical increment of this increase will be limited. The market is likely to discount the headline figure; actual additional supply reaching end-users will depend on Hormuz reopening progress. A formal OPEC+ emergency meeting announcement before March 31 would be a significant catalyst (possible if Brent falls below $85).

- [Mar 26–28, 2026] Ongoing Israeli airstrikes on Iranian gas/energy infrastructure (event risk, indeterminate timing) — Actor: Israel / Iran
  Israeli military operations against Iranian gas infrastructure have continued daily since February 28. Each new confirmed strike on a major facility has historically triggered $3–$8 upside spikes in Brent within the same session. Conversely, any ceasefire announcement or confirmed humanitarian corridor for tanker traffic would trigger an immediate $8–$15 downside move. No fixed schedule; event risk is continuous throughout the 7-day window.
