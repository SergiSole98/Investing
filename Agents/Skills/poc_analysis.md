# POC analysis

## Context
Applies when a support and resistance level derived from POC data are close enough that a single candle can cover the full distance between them.

## Rules

1. **When a support and resistance are within ~0.5–1% of each other, classify the zone as congestion — not independent levels.**
2. A congestion zone has no real directional room: touching the support puts the resistance immediately overhead, and vice versa. Do NOT issue a directional signal from it.
3. The zone indicates the asset has repeatedly reversed in both directions from the same price area — this is indecision, not a clean bounce setup.
4. Expect one of two outcomes: prolonged sideways oscillation until a catalyst appears, or a violent breakout driven by accumulated stops on both sides of the zone.
5. Treat a breakout above resistance or below support as the actionable signal, not the touch of either level in isolation.
