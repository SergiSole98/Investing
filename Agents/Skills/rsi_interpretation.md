# RSI interpretation

## Context
Applies when interpreting RSI(14) signals on 4h–7 day horizons. Covers level reading, divergences, failure swings, and confluence rules.

## Rules

### Core levels
1. RSI oscillates 0–100. Above 50 is bullish bias; below 50 is bearish bias.
2. 70 = overbought zone. 30 = oversold zone. Neither triggers an automatic trade.
3. In a strong uptrend, RSI typically holds 40–90 with support near 40–50. In a downtrend, it holds 10–60 with resistance near 50–60.
4. High RSI in a strong trend signals continuation, not reversal.

### Divergences
5. Bearish divergence: price prints higher highs, RSI does not → potential weakening.
6. Bullish divergence: price prints lower lows, RSI does not → potential bounce.
7. **Only act on divergences when they appear on higher timeframes, after an extended move, and at a key support or resistance level.**

### Failure swings
8. Bullish failure swing: RSI drops below 30, rebounds, holds above 30 on the next dip, then breaks its prior swing high.
9. Bearish failure swing: RSI rises above 70, pulls back, fails to reclaim 70, then breaks its prior swing low.
10. Failure swings are self-contained RSI signals — price action confirms, but the signal originates within the indicator.

### Parameter adjustment
11. Period 7–10: more sensitive, more noise. Period 21–30: smoother, fewer signals.
12. Adapt period to timeframe. Do not use the same setting across intraday and swing charts.

### Confluence and confirmation
13. **Never use RSI in isolation.** Confirm with price action (rejections, candle patterns, breakouts), structure, and volume.
14. In the 4h–7 day system, prioritize RSI 14 on 4h and 1D.
15. In uptrends, look for pullbacks to RSI 40–50 as entry zones, not overbought readings as exits.
16. Use 50-line crossovers as bias confirmation, not entry signals.
17. Use divergences only when they coincide with a key S/R zone and market structure supports the read.

### Common errors
18. Do NOT sell automatically above 70 or buy automatically below 30.
19. Do NOT ignore the primary trend when reading RSI levels.
20. Do NOT apply identical RSI thresholds across different assets or timeframes.
