## News Sources for Investment Analysis

Recognized media and specialized sources for impact-driven news research.

### Wire Services

- **Associated Press (AP)** — General news, wire distribution
- **Reuters** — Financial and general news, wire distribution

### Financial & Markets

- **Bloomberg** — Markets, finance, corporate news
- **Wall Street Journal** — Finance, business, markets
- **Financial Times** — Global finance, markets, business
- **CNBC** — Financial television and digital
- **Benzinga** — Trading and market news

### Commodity-Specific

- **S&P Global Platts** — Energy, metals, commodities pricing and analysis
- **Oil & Gas Journal** — Energy industry reporting
- **EIA (US Energy Information Administration)** — US energy data and analysis

### Geopolitical & Macro

- **BBC News** — Global news, geopolitical reporting
- **The Economist** — Economics, geopolitics, markets analysis
- **Barron's** — Markets, stocks, wealth management

### Specialized Investor Research

- **Seeking Alpha** — Stock analysis and investor community
- **Investor's Business Daily (IBD)** — Stock and market analysis
- **Morningstar** — Fund and stock research
- **The Block** — Crypto and digital assets

## Application Rules

1. Prioritize Tier 1 sources (Wire Services, Bloomberg, Reuters, WSJ) for breaking news and official announcements.
2. Use Tier 2 sources (Financial & Markets, specialized) for context, analysis, and asset-specific impact.
3. Include geopolitical and macro sources (BBC, The Economist) when relevant to the analyzed asset's risk factors.
4. Cross-reference Tier 3 (Seeking Alpha, Morningstar, IBD) for investor consensus or thematic impact.
5. Do not include unvetted blogs, social media, or unconfirmed rumors.
6. Verify publication source name against this list before including in analysis.
