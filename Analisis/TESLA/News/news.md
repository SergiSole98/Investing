- [2026-04-20] Reuters Investigation: Tesla Shifted $18 Billion Offshore, Paying Zero Federal Tax in 2025 — Source: Reuters
  Reuters examined corporate filings across 14 countries and found Tesla reported a federal tax bill of $0 for 2025, continuing a pattern where, in all but one of the past 20 years, the company declared owing no U.S. taxes despite $264 billion in cumulative U.S. revenues. Tesla subsidiaries in the Netherlands and Singapore booked $18 billion in profits between 2023 and early 2025, with the Singapore entity reporting 89% of Tesla's global operating income while selling only 6,633 cars locally. Three tax experts consulted by Reuters confirmed the arrangement appears designed for profit shifting; no illegal activity was alleged. The report highlighted a contrast with Elon Musk's prior public statements rejecting tax loopholes as "shady"; neither Tesla nor Musk responded to requests for comment.

- [2026-04-18] Tesla Launches Unsupervised Robotaxi Service in Dallas and Houston — Source: Reuters (wire, cited by U.S. News & World Report / multiple outlets)
  On April 18, 2026, Tesla activated fully unsupervised robotaxi rides in Dallas and Houston, Texas, with no human safety monitor aboard — the first multi-market commercial robotaxi deployment without any safety driver by a major U.S. operator. The service operates within defined geofenced zones (approximately 25 square miles each) and is accessible via the Tesla app. Tesla deployed 573 vehicles across both cities; the TSLA stock surged approximately 12% over four trading sessions around the announcement. CEO Elon Musk stated Tesla aims to have robotaxis operating in dozens of U.S. cities by end of 2026, with planned near-term expansion to Las Vegas, Miami, Orlando, Phoenix, and Tampa.

- [2026-04-15] Tesla AI5 Chip Completes Tape-Out; Production Targeted for H2 2026 — Source: Electrek / Teslarati / NotATeslaApp
  On April 15, 2026, Elon Musk confirmed Tesla's AI5 chip reached tape-out, a key design-finalization milestone roughly two years behind the original schedule. The AI5 delivers up to 10x the compute of its predecessor (AI4) and is claimed to match NVIDIA H100 inference performance for Tesla's autonomous driving workloads. Initial production targets H2 2026, with deployment planned first for Optimus robots and supercomputer clusters rather than consumer vehicles (cars expected no earlier than 2027). Manufacturing partners include Samsung and TSMC. Tesla's AI chip roadmap also includes the successor AI6 and the Terafab compute facility, which Reuters and Bloomberg have reported is moving beyond the concept phase based on supplier contacts.
